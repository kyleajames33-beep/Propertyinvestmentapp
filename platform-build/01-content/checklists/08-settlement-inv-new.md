# Settlement Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Settlement day preparation
- [ ] Confirm settlement date and time with your conveyancer 3 days prior
- [ ] Verify loan funds are in place and your lender has issued all required documents
- [ ] Confirm the deposit is held in trust and the balance of funds is ready for transfer
- [ ] Review the settlement statement: adjustments for rates, water, strata, and rent
- [ ] Be available by phone on settlement day to resolve any last-minute issues

## Insurance activation
- [ ] Confirm building and landlord insurance is active from exchange date
- [ ] Verify the policy covers building replacement, landlord contents, rent default, and malicious damage
- [ ] Provide a certificate of currency to your conveyancer before settlement
- [ ] Ensure the insured entity matches the purchasing entity on the contract

## Final inspection
- [ ] Conduct final inspection within 48 hours of settlement
- [ ] Verify all inclusions are present and the property condition matches the contract
- [ ] If tenanted, introduce yourself to the tenant or confirm your property manager has done so
- [ ] Check that keys, remotes, and access devices are ready for handover
- [ ] Photograph meter readings at final inspection
- [ ] Report any issues to your conveyancer before settlement funds are released

## Tenancy handover
- [ ] Confirm your property manager has the tenant's contact details and lease documentation
- [ ] Transfer the bond to your name with the state bond authority
- [ ] Provide the property manager with the condition report and ingoing photos
- [ ] Confirm the tenant's rent payment method will continue or needs updating
- [ ] Instruct the property manager on your preferred maintenance approval threshold
- [ ] Review and sign the property management agreement with your chosen agency

## Utilities and ongoing management
- [ ] Transfer or set up electricity, gas, and water accounts: tenant may pay direct for some
- [ ] Confirm council rates billing is set up in your name
- [ ] Set up a dedicated bank account for rental income and property expenses
- [ ] Schedule annual landlord insurance review and building inspection reminders
- [ ] Diary key dates: lease expiry, rent review, depreciation report order, tax return deadlines

## Post-settlement setup
- [ ] Order a depreciation schedule from a quantity surveyor: do this as soon as possible after settlement
- [ ] Engage an accountant familiar with investment property tax if you do not already have one
- [ ] Set up a filing system for all receipts, invoices, and property-related documents
- [ ] Update your personal balance sheet to include the new asset and liability
- [ ] Review your loan online portal and set up automated repayments
- [ ] Confirm the title has been transferred correctly via a title search 2 weeks after settlement
