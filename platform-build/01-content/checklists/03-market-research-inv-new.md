# Market Research Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Market data collection
- [ ] Subscribe to CoreLogic, SQM Research or PropTrack for your target state or city
- [ ] Pull median price data for the last 10 years in each target suburb
- [ ] Calculate the compound annual growth rate (CAGR) for houses and units separately
- [ ] Track days on market trends: rising numbers often signal a slowing market
- [ ] Monitor auction clearance rates weekly for your target regions
- [ ] Set up automated alerts for new listings, price reductions and sold results in your target suburbs

## Supply and demand analysis
- [ ] Research the current number of listings versus the 5-year average for each suburb
- [ ] Check the development pipeline: DA-approved projects, construction commencements, completions
- [ ] Review population growth forecasts for the local government area from the state planning department
- [ ] Identify major employment hubs and transport projects that will drive demand
- [ ] Calculate the rental vacancy rate: aim for suburbs under 2.5%
- [ ] Research the rental yield spread between houses and units in the area
- [ ] Check interstate migration data if targeting a city you do not live in

## Rental market fundamentals
- [ ] Research the dominant tenant demographic: families, young professionals, students, retirees
- [ ] Check average weekly rents for your target property type over the last 3 years
- [ ] Calculate gross and net rental yields using current median prices and rents
- [ ] Research typical lease terms: 6 months, 12 months, or longer: in the area
- [ ] Identify property features that rent fastest in the suburb: air conditioning, parking, outdoor space
- [ ] Call 3 local property managers for a rental appraisal and their read on the market
- [ ] Ask property managers about common tenant complaints and what properties lease fastest

## Infrastructure and growth drivers
- [ ] Check state and federal infrastructure budgets for projects in or near your target suburbs
- [ ] Review council strategic plans for zoning changes, density increases, or new commercial precincts
- [ ] Map major employers, hospitals, universities and transport interchanges
- [ ] Check the NBN status and any planned digital infrastructure upgrades
- [ ] Research any planned road upgrades that could improve or degrade property values

## On-ground verification
- [ ] Visit each target suburb in person or send a trusted representative
- [ ] Walk the main streets, check the retail mix, and assess the general condition of properties
- [ ] Attend local auctions to gauge competition levels and buyer sentiment
- [ ] Meet with 2-3 local property managers to compare their fees, services and market knowledge
- [ ] Drive through the suburb at different times of day to assess street activity and safety
