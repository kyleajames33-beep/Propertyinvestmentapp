# Contract Review Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Conveyancer and structure
- [ ] Confirm your conveyancer has experience with investment property transactions
- [ ] Verify the purchasing entity on the contract matches your chosen ownership structure
- [ ] Provide the contract at least 5 business days before intended exchange
- [ ] Confirm their fees and turnaround time for review and advice

## Standard contract review
- [ ] Verify the property address, legal description, and purchase price are correct
- [ ] Check the deposit: typically 10%: and negotiate 5% if cash flow is tight
- [ ] Review the settlement period and confirm it aligns with your finance approval
- [ ] Check the inclusions and exclusions list: fixtures, fittings, appliances
- [ ] Confirm vacant possession or subject to existing leases: critical for rental continuity
- [ ] Review the cooling off period provisions in NSW

## Special conditions
- [ ] Include a finance clause: 14-21 days: unless unconditionally approved
- [ ] Include a building and pest inspection clause
- [ ] Add a subject to satisfactory lease verification clause if tenanted
- [ ] Include a final inspection clause within 48 hours of settlement
- [ ] Add a special condition requiring the vendor to transfer any remaining prepayments: council rates, water
- [ ] If buying off-the-plan, review the sunset clause and your rights if the project delays

## Tenancy documentation
- [ ] Attach the current lease as a schedule to the contract
- [ ] Verify the rent amount, lease term, and any special conditions
- [ ] Confirm the tenant has paid rent up to the settlement date
- [ ] Check that the bond is correctly lodged and will be transferred to you at settlement
- [ ] Review the condition report and ingoing photos
- [ ] Confirm the vendor has served all required notices and there are no disputes

## Searches and certificates
- [ ] Order a title search to confirm ownership and any encumbrances
- [ ] Obtain Section 10.7 (2) and (5) certificates: zoning, hazards, restrictions
- [ ] Check for easements, covenants, and caveats on the title
- [ ] Order a drainage diagram and confirm no sewer easements affect the block
- [ ] Verify no outstanding council notices or orders
- [ ] Confirm land tax clearance if buying from an investor
- [ ] Order a strata report if buying a unit, townhouse, or any community title property

## Insurance
- [ ] Arrange building and landlord insurance effective from exchange date
- [ ] Confirm the policy covers building replacement, landlord contents, rent default, and tenant damage
- [ ] Check for exclusions related to the property's location or condition
- [ ] Provide a certificate of currency to your conveyancer before settlement
