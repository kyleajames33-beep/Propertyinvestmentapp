# Shortlisting Checklist: Experienced Investor

## Acquisition screening
- [ ] Set hard filters: max price, min yield, max vacancy rate: and eliminate anything that fails
- [ ] Run a 2-minute comparable sales check on nearby sold properties in the last 90 days
- [ ] Calculate immediate yield and projected yield after any planned improvements
- [ ] Model cash flow impact on your portfolio: does this property improve or drag your overall position?
- [ ] Check whether the property aligns with your current portfolio gaps: location, type, or tenant segment

## Deal analysis
- [ ] Estimate all-in acquisition cost including stamp duty, legals, inspections, and any immediate works
- [ ] Calculate the cap rate if commercial, or gross and net yield if residential
- [ ] Assess value-add potential: renovation, subdivision, granny flat, rezoning, or cosmetic uplift
- [ ] Review the property's rental history if leased: consistency, tenant quality, and rent progression
- [ ] Check lease expiry dates and tenant negotiation leverage if tenanted

## Rapid due diligence
- [ ] Verify zoning and any planning constraints on the council portal
- [ ] Check for nearby DAs or infrastructure that could impact value
- [ ] Confirm no outstanding orders, encumbrances, or easements that would restrict use
- [ ] Brief your property manager for a rental appraisal and their gut feel on the deal
- [ ] Cross-check the agent's rental estimate against your own research and the manager's opinion

## Portfolio fit check
- [ ] Confirm the purchase does not push your portfolio LVR above your ceiling
- [ ] Model the impact on your land tax bill across all states
- [ ] Assess whether this property diversifies or concentrates your risk
- [ ] Lock in your maximum offer before negotiating: do not wing it
