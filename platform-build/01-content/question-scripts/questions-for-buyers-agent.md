# Questions to Ask a Buyer's Agent

## Why These Questions Matter

A buyer's agent represents you in the property purchase, not the seller. They find properties, assess value, negotiate on your behalf, and can bid at auction. A skilled buyer's agent can save you money and prevent costly mistakes. An unskilled or conflicted one can cost you more than their fee.

A good answer demonstrates deep local knowledge, references specific recent transactions, and includes a clear fee structure with no hidden costs. The buyer's agent works for you. You are the decision-maker, and you need to know exactly how they operate before you sign any agreement.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Licensing:** In NSW, buyer's agents must hold a real estate licence or certificate of registration issued by NSW Fair Trading. Verify on the NSW Fair Trading licence check.
- **Membership:** Look for membership with the Real Estate Institute of NSW (REINSW) or the Real Estate Buyers Agents Association of Australia (REBAA). REBAA members are exclusively buyer's agents and do not sell property.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews, ProductReview.com.au, and ask for recent client references specific to your target suburbs.
- **Exclusive representation:** Confirm they operate solely as buyer's agents and do not also work as selling agents, which creates a conflict of interest.

## Questions by Category

### Buyer Representation Agreement

1. What is the term of your buyer representation agreement, and what happens if I want to terminate early? Is there a cooling-off period?
2. Does your agreement include an exclusivity clause that prevents me from viewing or purchasing properties independently during our engagement?
3. What are my obligations under the agreement, and what are yours? Specifically, what happens if you present a property that meets my criteria but I choose not to proceed?

### Fee Structure

4. Is your fee a fixed amount, a percentage of the purchase price, or a combination? What was the average fee paid by your clients in the last 12 months?
5. If your fee is percentage-based, how do you prevent a conflict of interest where you might prefer a higher purchase price to increase your fee?
6. Is the engagement fee payable upfront, and is it refundable if you do not find a suitable property within the agreed timeframe?
7. Are there additional charges for auction bidding, travel to inspect properties, or post-purchase coordination with my conveyancer and broker?

### Independence from Selling Agents

8. Do you accept referral fees or commissions from selling agents, developers, or property marketers for introducing buyers?
9. How do you ensure the properties you recommend are the best fit for me, rather than properties where the selling agent is motivated to close a deal quickly?
10. What percentage of the properties you purchased for clients in the last 12 months were off-market or pre-market, versus listed on Domain and Realestate.com.au?

### Suburb Knowledge and Research

11. Which suburbs do you specialise in, and how many properties have you purchased in those suburbs in the last 24 months?
12. What research do you conduct before recommending a suburb or specific property? Do you analyse recent comparable sales, rental yields, vacancy rates, and planned infrastructure?
13. Can you walk me through a property you purchased in my target suburb in the last six months, including the listed price, the purchase price, and why you recommended it?

### Negotiation Approach

14. What is your negotiation strategy for properties sold by private treaty versus auction? Can you describe a recent negotiation where you secured a property below the vendor's reserve or below comparable sales?
15. If a property goes to auction, will you bid on my behalf? What is your experience with auction bidding, and what is your success rate?

### Refund Policy

16. If we do not purchase a property within the agreed timeframe, what happens to the fees I have paid? Is any portion refunded, or does it roll over to an extended engagement?
17. If I am unhappy with your service, what is your dispute resolution process? Have you ever had a complaint lodged with NSW Fair Trading or the Property and Stock Agents Regulatory body?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **Also operates as a selling agent or works for an agency that does both.** This creates a fundamental conflict of interest. Look for a dedicated buyer's agent.
- **Accepts referral fees from developers or selling agents.** This means they may be motivated to sell you a specific property rather than find the best one for you.
- **Cannot name specific recent purchases** in your target suburb with prices and outcomes. General claims about "great results" are not enough.
- **Pressure to sign the agreement immediately** without time to review the terms or compare agents.
- **Fee is only payable on success, with no upfront component.** While this sounds attractive, it can mean the agent is not invested in a thorough search or is relying on volume rather than quality.
- **No clear answer on how they handle conflicts of interest** or how they are remunerated beyond your fee.
- **Guarantees a purchase price** or claims they can "always beat the market." No professional can guarantee this.

---

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is the total cost to me if you successfully purchase a property at $[my budget]? Break this down into engagement fee, search fee, and success fee.
- Is the success fee calculated on the purchase price or the listed price? What happens if the property sells above the listing range?
- Are there any circumstances where I would pay additional fees beyond what is in the written agreement?
- Do you offer a tiered fee structure for different service levels (e.g. full search and negotiation versus auction bidding only)?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Request the contact details of two recent clients** who purchased in your target area. Call them and ask about their experience.
2. **Review the buyer representation agreement carefully.** Look for hidden fees, automatic renewal clauses, and restrictive exclusivity terms. Consider having your solicitor review it before signing.
3. **Check their recent purchase history.** Ask for a list of the last 10 properties they purchased, including suburbs and approximate purchase prices.
4. **Compare two other buyer's agents** before making your decision. Fee structures and service levels vary significantly.
5. **Verify they have no affiliations** with developers or property marketers that could influence their recommendations. Ask directly and check their website for any marketing partnerships.
