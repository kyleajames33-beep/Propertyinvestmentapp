# Market Research Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Data sources and platforms
- [ ] Set up saved searches on Domain and realestate.com.au for your target suburbs and price range
- [ ] Create a spreadsheet to track every property you view: address, price, bedrooms, days on market, outcome
- [ ] Subscribe to NSW's property market report: SQM Research, CoreLogic, or PropTrack
- [ ] Check the ABS Census data for your target suburbs: demographics, income levels, household composition
- [ ] Follow local community Facebook groups to understand neighbourhood dynamics and common complaints

## Suburb analysis
- [ ] Research median house and unit prices for your target suburbs over the last 5 years
- [ ] Compare the suburb's median to neighbouring suburbs: identify value gaps
- [ ] Check the suburb's price growth rate over 1 year, 5 years, and 10 years
- [ ] Look at days on market averages: a rising number can signal softening demand
- [ ] Review auction clearance rates for the suburb and the wider region
- [ ] Research rental yields even if you plan to live there: high yields often signal investor demand and future growth

## Infrastructure and amenities
- [ ] Check transport links: train stations, bus routes, motorways, cycle paths: and planned upgrades
- [ ] Identify schools in the catchment and check their My School ratings
- [ ] Map proximity to hospitals, GP clinics, pharmacies and emergency services
- [ ] Research shopping centres, supermarkets, cafes and entertainment options
- [ ] Check council websites for planned infrastructure projects: roads, parks, community facilities
- [ ] Look up the National Broadband Network (NBN) availability and technology type for the suburb
- [ ] Research crime statistics via the state police website or local council reports

## Supply and demand fundamentals
- [ ] Check how many properties are currently listed for sale in the suburb: is supply rising or falling?
- [ ] Research the pipeline of new developments: apartments, housing estates, townhouse projects
- [ ] Look at population growth projections for the local government area
- [ ] Check employment drivers in the region: major employers, industry diversity, job growth
- [ ] Review vacancy rates for rental properties: under 2% typically signals tight supply
- [ ] Research zoning rules that might allow higher-density development near your target streets

## On-ground visits
- [ ] Visit your top 3 suburbs at different times: weekday morning, Saturday afternoon, weekday evening
- [ ] Drive or walk the streets you are considering at night to assess noise, lighting and safety
- [ ] Chat to locals at cafes or parks about what it is like to live there
- [ ] Time your commute to work during peak hours from each suburb
- [ ] Visit local shops and assess their condition, variety and price levels
- [ ] Check the condition of public spaces: parks, footpaths, rubbish collection, street maintenance
- [ ] Attend at least 2 auctions in the suburb before you are ready to bid: observe bidder behaviour and prices
