# Privacy policy outline

## Purpose

This document provides an outline for the platform's privacy policy. It is structured to comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. The outline also addresses Spam Act 2003 (Cth) compliance for email communications.

**Important:** This is an outline only. Sections marked [REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] must be reviewed by a solicitor qualified in Australian privacy law and tailored to the platform's specific data handling practices before publication.

## Part 2: what personal information we collect

### 2.1 Types of personal information collected

We may collect the following types of personal information:

- **Identity information:** name, title, date of birth
- **Contact information:** email address, phone number, residential address, postal address
- **Property interests:** locations you are interested in, property type preferences, budget range, intended purchase timeline, whether you are a first home buyer, investor or owner-occupier
- **Financial information:** your stated income range, deposit amount, pre-approval status (only if voluntarily provided)
- **Professional service needs:** whether you are seeking a mortgage broker, conveyancer, property inspector, financial adviser, buyer's agent or other professional
- **Device and usage information:** IP address, browser type, operating system, pages visited, time spent on pages, referring website, click patterns
- **Communication records:** emails, form submissions, chat transcripts, phone call notes
- **Marketing preferences:** your opt-in and opt-out preferences for marketing communications

We generally do not collect sensitive information as defined in the Privacy Act. If we do collect sensitive information (for example, if you voluntarily disclose health information relevant to your property needs), we will only do so with your consent or where otherwise permitted by law. See APP 3.3-3.4.

### 2.2 Anonymity and pseudonymity

Where practicable, you may interact with us without identifying yourself or using a pseudonym. For example, you can browse our website without providing personal information. However, if you wish to use certain features (such as requesting a referral or subscribing to communications), we will need to collect your contact details. See APP 2.

## Part 3: how we collect personal information

### 3.1 Direct collection

We collect personal information directly from you when you:

- complete a registration or enquiry form on our website
- request a referral to a professional service provider
- subscribe to our newsletter or property alerts
- contact us by email, phone or through our website contact form
- participate in surveys or provide feedback
- use interactive tools on our website (calculators, assessments)
- communicate with us via social media platforms

### 3.2 Automated collection

We collect certain information automatically when you visit our website through:

- **Cookies and similar technologies:** small files stored on your device that help us remember your preferences and understand how you use our site. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.
- **Analytics services:** we use analytics tools to collect information about website traffic and user behaviour. This information is generally aggregated and does not identify you personally.
- **Server logs:** our servers automatically record information including your IP address, browser type, access times and pages viewed.

### 3.3 Collection from third parties

In some cases, we may collect personal information from third parties, such as:

- professional service providers who refer you to us (with your consent)
- publicly available sources (such as property listing websites, where you have made your interest public)
- our marketing and analytics partners

When we collect personal information from third parties, we will take reasonable steps to notify you of the collection in accordance with APP 5. See APP 5.1 and the APP Guidelines on reasonable steps for notification.

## Part 4: purpose of collection

### 4.1 Primary purposes

We collect personal information for the following primary purposes:

- to provide you with information, tools and resources about property and related services
- to refer you to professional service providers (such as mortgage brokers, conveyancers, property inspectors, buyer's agents, financial advisers) that match your stated needs and preferences
- to communicate with you about your enquiries, referrals and use of our services
- to improve our website, tools and user experience

### 4.2 Secondary purposes

We may also use your personal information for secondary purposes that are related to the primary purpose and that you would reasonably expect, including:

- to send you property market updates, newsletters and educational content (where you have consented or where permitted under the Spam Act and APP 7)
- to analyse usage patterns and improve our platform functionality
- to conduct market research and compile aggregated statistics
- to comply with our legal obligations and resolve any disputes
- to protect our rights, property and safety and that of our users

### 4.3 Direct marketing

We may use your personal information to send you direct marketing communications about property-related services, market updates and referral partner offerings. We will only send you direct marketing communications:

- where you have consented to receive them; or
- where an exception under APP 7 applies.

Every direct marketing communication will include a clear opt-out mechanism. If you opt out, we will stop sending you marketing communications within 5 working days. See the Spam Act compliance section below and APP 7.

## Part 5: how we share personal information with referral partners

### 5.1 Sharing with referral partners

When you request a referral through our platform, we will share your personal information with the relevant professional service provider so they can contact you and provide their services. The information shared may include:

- your name, phone number and email address
- your property interests and preferences
- your stated service needs
- any additional information you have provided that is relevant to the referral

We only share this information with your consent, obtained at the time you request the referral. See APP 6 (use or disclosure with consent).

### 5.2 Referral partner obligations

Our referral partners are independent professional service providers. They are not our employees or agents. Each referral partner has their own privacy policy and is responsible for complying with the Privacy Act in their handling of your personal information.

We take reasonable steps to ensure that referral partners understand they must handle your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act. However, we are not responsible for the privacy practices of referral partners once your information has been shared with them. You should review the privacy policy of any referral partner before engaging their services.

### 5.3 Other disclosures

We may also disclose your personal information:

- to our service providers who help us operate the platform (such as hosting providers, email delivery services, analytics providers, customer relationship management systems)
- where required or authorised by law (for example, in response to a court order or request from a law enforcement agency)
- to our professional advisers (accountants, lawyers, insurers) as necessary
- if there is a change of control in our business or a sale of assets, to the prospective purchaser on a confidential basis

### 5.4 Overseas disclosure

Some of our service providers are located overseas. In particular:

- our website contact form and email delivery services are hosted by Formspree Inc., which stores data in the United States
- our analytics and cloud storage providers may process data in the United States, Singapore and other countries

When we disclose your personal information to overseas recipients, we take reasonable steps to ensure that the recipient does not breach the Australian Privacy Principles in relation to that information. See APP 8.1. This includes:

- entering into contractual arrangements that require the overseas recipient to comply with the APPs
- verifying that the overseas recipient is subject to a law or binding scheme that has the effect of protecting personal information in a substantially similar way to the APPs
- obtaining your consent to the overseas disclosure

The countries in which overseas recipients are likely to be located include the United States and Singapore. ## Part 6: storage and security

### 6.1 Data storage

We store personal information in electronic format using cloud-based services. Our data is stored on servers located in Australia and the United States. We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal information is protected from misuse, interference and loss, and from unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. See APP 11.

### 6.2 Security measures

Our security measures include:

- encryption of data in transit using SSL/TLS protocols
- password protection and multi-factor authentication for our systems
- access controls limiting who can access personal information
- regular security reviews and updates
- staff training on privacy and data security

### 6.3 Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal, accounting or reporting requirements. When personal information is no longer needed, we take reasonable steps to destroy it or de-identify it in a secure manner. See APP 11.2.

### 6.4 Data breaches

If we become aware of a data breach involving your personal information that is likely to result in serious harm to you, we will:

- notify you as soon as practicable
- notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)
- take steps to contain the breach and prevent further harm

This is required under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme in Part IIIC of the Privacy Act. See APP 11 and the OAIC's Data Breach Preparation and Response guide.

## Part 7: access and correction

### 7.1 Access

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you. We will respond to access requests within a reasonable time (generally within 30 days for organisations). There is no fee for making an access request, but we may charge a reasonable fee for processing the request if it is complex or time-consuming. See APP 12.

If we refuse to give you access, we will give you written reasons for the refusal and information about how you can complain about the decision.

### 7.2 Correction

If you believe that personal information we hold about you is incorrect, incomplete or out of date, you may request that we correct it. We will take reasonable steps to correct the information within a reasonable time. See APP 13.

If we refuse to correct your personal information, you may request that we associate a statement with the information noting that you believe it to be incorrect.

## Part 8: complaints

### 8.1 How to make a complaint

If you believe we have breached the Australian Privacy Principles or mishandled your personal information, you can make a complaint by contacting our Privacy Officer using the contact details above. We take all complaints seriously and will respond within a reasonable time.

### 8.2 Our complaint handling process

When we receive a privacy complaint, we will:

- acknowledge receipt of the complaint within a reasonable time
- investigate the complaint and gather relevant information
- provide you with a written response setting out our findings and any action we will take
- aim to resolve the complaint within 30 days

If you are not satisfied with our response, you can escalate your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

## Part 9: cookies and online tracking

### 9.1 Types of cookies we use

We use the following types of cookies:

- **Essential cookies:** required for the website to function properly (for example, maintaining your session)
- **Analytics cookies:** help us understand how visitors interact with our website
- **Preference cookies:** remember your settings and preferences for future visits

### 9.2 Managing cookies

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. You can choose to accept or reject cookies. Note that rejecting some cookies may affect the functionality of the website.

### 9.3 Third party analytics

We use analytics services (such as Google Analytics) to collect information about website traffic and usage patterns. These services may use cookies and similar technologies. The information collected is generally aggregated and anonymised. For more information about how these services handle data, please refer to their respective privacy policies.

## Part 10: Spam Act compliance

### 10.1 Consent for commercial electronic messages

We comply with the Spam Act 2003 (Cth) and the Spam Regulations 2021. We will only send you commercial electronic messages (including emails and SMS) with your consent or where another exception under the Spam Act applies. See ACMA guidance on consent and the Spam Act.

Types of consent include:

- **Express consent:** you have clearly agreed to receive messages (for example, by ticking a box on a form or subscribing to a newsletter)
- **Inferred consent:** we have an existing business relationship and it is reasonable to believe you would expect to receive messages from us

### 10.2 Message requirements

All commercial electronic messages we send will:

- clearly identify us as the sender
- include accurate contact details
- contain a functional unsubscribe facility that is actioned within 5 working days

### 10.3 Unsubscribe

Every marketing email we send includes an unsubscribe link. You can unsubscribe at any time. If you unsubscribe, we will stop sending you marketing messages within 5 working days, as required by the Spam Act and ACMA guidance. See the ACMA fact sheet on email and SMS unsubscribe rules.

Note that even if you unsubscribe from marketing messages, we may still send you transactional messages related to your use of our services (such as referral confirmations and account notifications).

When we make material changes, we will notify you by posting an updated version on our website and updating the "last reviewed" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
