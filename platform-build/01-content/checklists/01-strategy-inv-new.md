# Strategy Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Investment strategy
- [ ] Define your investment goal: capital growth, rental yield, or a blend: and write it down
- [ ] Choose your strategy type: buy and hold, renovation, build-to-rent, or NDIS/SFR
- [ ] Set your minimum target yield and your minimum annual growth expectation
- [ ] Decide your hold period: minimum years before you would consider selling
- [ ] Confirm whether negative gearing suits your current taxable income position
- [ ] Model your cash flow: rental income minus loan interest, rates, insurance, maintenance, management fees, vacancies

## Finance structure
- [ ] Speak to an accountant about the right ownership structure: personal, joint, trust, or SMSF
- [ ] Understand the implications of cross-collateralisation and decide whether to avoid it
- [ ] Calculate your maximum borrowing capacity across your existing home and the new purchase
- [ ] Confirm your deposit source: equity release, cash savings, or both
- [ ] Get a valuation on your existing property if you plan to access equity
- [ ] Research lender requirements for investment loans: higher deposits, rate premiums, servicing buffers
- [ ] Factor in lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) if your deposit is under 20% of the purchase price

## Tax and structure considerations
- [ ] Confirm what expenses are deductible versus non-deductible for investment properties
- [ ] Understand depreciation schedules and what can be claimed: capital works and plant and equipment
- [ ] Register for an ABN if you plan to manage the property through a business structure
- [ ] Budget for landlord insurance, building insurance, and contents cover for fittings
- [ ] Research land tax thresholds in the state you are buying in and how they apply to investments

## Market and criteria
- [ ] Define your target market: capital city, regional, or interstate: and justify the choice
- [ ] Set your maximum purchase price based on your pre-approval and cash flow model
- [ ] Identify 3-5 suburbs that match your yield and growth criteria
- [ ] Research vacancy rates, rental demand, and typical lease terms in each target suburb
- [ ] Check planned infrastructure projects, zoning changes, and development pipelines
- [ ] Set a rule to inspect at least 20 properties in your target area before making an offer
