# Inspection & Due Diligence Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Building and structural inspection
- [ ] Engage a licensed building inspector for a full pre-purchase inspection
- [ ] Review the report for structural defects, moisture damage, roofing condition, and electrical safety
- [ ] Get cost estimates for any major repairs and factor them into your offer or cash flow model
- [ ] Check the property's build date: pre-1980s properties may contain asbestos
- [ ] Inspect for water damage, plumbing issues, and inadequate drainage
- [ ] Verify the condition of kitchens and bathrooms: these drive rental appeal and are expensive to replace

## Pest inspection
- [ ] Book a pest and termite inspection with a licensed operator
- [ ] Check for active termite damage, previous treatments, and the presence of termite barriers
- [ ] Factor annual pest inspection costs into your property expenses
- [ ] Ask the inspector about the termite risk profile for the suburb

## Strata and body corporate (if applicable)
- [ ] Order a strata inspection report covering the last 3 years of records
- [ ] Check levy amounts, special levies, sinking fund balance, and capital works plans
- [ ] Review AGM minutes for disputes, maintenance backlogs, or governance issues
- [ ] Check the strata manager's track record and responsiveness
- [ ] Confirm building insurance is adequate and up to date
- [ ] Assess whether the building has defects or cladding issues that could trigger large special levies

## Tenancy and income verification
- [ ] Request a copy of the current lease agreement: check expiry date, rent amount, and terms
- [ ] Verify the rent matches or exceeds market rates: under-market leases reduce your yield
- [ ] Check the tenant's payment history: are they in arrears or consistently late?
- [ ] Review the condition report and ingoing photos from when the current tenant moved in
- [ ] Confirm the bond is lodged with the state bond authority and matches the lease terms
- [ ] Ask the property manager about the tenant's history and likelihood of renewal

## Compliance and legal checks
- [ ] Obtain Section 10.7 (2) and (5) certificates from council
- [ ] Check zoning, land use restrictions, and any proposed rezonings
- [ ] Verify all structures have appropriate approvals and complying development certificates
- [ ] Check for easements, restrictive covenants, or caveats on the title
- [ ] Confirm smoke alarm compliance and electrical safety switch requirements for NSW
- [ ] Check pool safety compliance if the property has a pool

## Financial due diligence
- [ ] Confirm council rates, water rates, and any strata or body corporate levies
- [ ] Get landlord insurance quotes: building, contents, and rent default cover
- [ ] Verify land tax liability for the property at the purchase price
- [ ] Calculate the exact cash flow position using confirmed costs and rental income
- [ ] Confirm you can claim depreciation and order a quantity surveyor report if needed
- [ ] Review the depreciation schedule from the previous owner if available
