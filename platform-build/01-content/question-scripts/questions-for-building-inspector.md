# Questions to Ask a Building Inspector

## Why These Questions Matter

A building inspection is one of the most important pre-purchase checks you will undertake. A thorough inspection can reveal structural defects, moisture problems, illegal building work, and safety hazards that could cost tens of thousands of dollars to repair. A rushed or superficial inspection gives you false confidence and no protection when problems emerge after settlement.

A good answer references compliance with Australian Standards, specifies exactly what is and is not covered, and includes clear details on insurance and reporting. You are paying for a professional assessment that could save you from a major financial mistake. Treat the selection of an inspector with the same rigour as any other professional you hire.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before engaging an inspector:

- **Licensing:** In NSW, building inspectors who carry out pre-purchase inspections are required to hold a building consultant licence issued by NSW Fair Trading. Verify the licence on the NSW Fair Trading licence check tool.
- **Insurance:** Confirm they hold both professional indemnity insurance and public liability insurance. Ask for the coverage amounts.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews and ask for recent references from buyers who purchased properties similar to the one you are considering.
- **Qualifications:** Look for a licensed builder or someone with a tertiary qualification in building surveying. Experience as a builder adds practical knowledge of construction defects.

---

## Questions by Category

### Licensing

1. Do you hold a current building consultant licence issued by NSW Fair Trading, and what is your licence number?
2. What is your background and qualification? Are you a licensed builder, a building surveyor, or do you hold another relevant qualification? How many years have you been conducting pre-purchase inspections?
3. Are you a member of a professional body such as the Australian Society of Building Consultants or the Master Builders Association? What ongoing training do you complete?

### Insurance

4. What is the value of your professional indemnity insurance, and does it specifically cover pre-purchase building inspections?
5. Do you hold public liability insurance, and what is the coverage amount? What happens if you damage the property during the inspection?
6. If you miss a major defect that should have been identified, what is your process for handling the claim? Have you ever had a claim made against your professional indemnity insurance?

### Australian Standard Compliance

7. Do you conduct your inspections in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1? Can you confirm this will be stated in your report?
8. Does your inspection include checking for compliance with the Building Code of Australia and relevant NSW legislation, or do you only assess the visible condition of the building?
9. Do you use moisture metres, thermal imaging cameras, or other diagnostic tools as part of your standard inspection, or are these only available as add-on services?

### Scope of Inspection

10. What areas of the property are excluded from your standard inspection? For example, do you inspect the roof void, the subfloor, the boundaries of the property, and retaining walls?
11. How long does a standard inspection of a [property type, e.g. three-bedroom freestanding house, two-bedroom apartment] typically take? What happens if you need more time on site?
12. If the property has been renovated or extended, do you check whether the work was completed with council approval and complies with current standards?

### Report Format and Turnaround

13. What format is your report delivered in, and how soon after the inspection will I receive it? Is it a standard template or tailored to the specific property?
14. Does the report include photographs of defects and a clear indication of which issues are urgent, which are serious, and which are minor maintenance items?
15. If I have questions after receiving the report, are you available to discuss your findings by phone? Is this included in the fee or charged separately?

### Pest Inspection

16. Is a timber pest inspection included in your standard fee, or is it a separate service? If separate, do you coordinate a licensed pest inspector, or do I need to engage one independently?
17. If you offer a combined building and pest inspection, is the pest inspection conducted by a licensed pest management technician who issues a separate pest report?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **No NSW Fair Trading building consultant licence** or the licence is expired or has conditions.
- **Inspection takes less than 30 minutes** for a standard three-bedroom house. A thorough inspection requires time.
- **No professional indemnity insurance** or coverage below $1 million. This is your only protection if they miss a major defect.
- **Report is a generic checklist** with no photographs and no specific references to the property you are buying.
- **Refuses to allow you to attend the inspection** or discourages your presence on site. A confident inspector welcomes client attendance.
- **Cannot explain what is excluded** from the inspection. Every inspection has limitations, and a professional will explain them upfront.
- **Quotes a price significantly below competitors** without being able to explain what is different about their service. This usually means corners are being cut.

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your total fee for a building inspection of a [property type] in [suburb], including GST?
- What is the additional cost for a timber pest inspection? Is there a discount for booking both together?
- Are there additional charges for properties over a certain size, properties with pools, or properties more than a certain age?
- What is your cancellation policy if the purchase does not proceed before the inspection date? Is any portion of the fee refundable?
- Do you charge extra for reports required within 24 hours, or is your standard turnaround already within that timeframe?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Verify the building consultant licence** on the NSW Fair Trading website. Check for any disciplinary actions or conditions.
2. **Request a sample report** from a recent inspection of a similar property. This shows you the quality and detail you can expect.
3. **Confirm the insurance details** by requesting a certificate of currency for both professional indemnity and public liability.
4. **Book the inspection with sufficient time** before your cooling-off period expires or before the auction date.
5. **Attend the inspection if possible.** Walking through the property with the inspector gives you context that a report alone cannot provide.
