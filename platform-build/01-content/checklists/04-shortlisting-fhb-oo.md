# Shortlisting Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Property scorecard setup
- [ ] Create a scorecard with your non-negotiables weighted by importance: location, bedrooms, budget, parking
- [ ] Set a scoring system out of 10 for each criterion so you can compare properties objectively
- [ ] Add a deal-breaker column to your scorecard: factors that immediately disqualify a property
- [ ] Test your scorecard on the first 3 properties you inspect and refine it if needed

## Comparison spreadsheet
- [ ] Build a spreadsheet with columns for address, price, land size, floor area, bedrooms, bathrooms, parking
- [ ] Add columns for distance to work, schools, public transport, shops and family
- [ ] Include a column for body corporate fees or strata levies if looking at apartments or townhouses
- [ ] Add a column for council rates estimate based on the property's land value
- [ ] Include a column for estimated monthly mortgage repayment at your pre-approved rate
- [ ] Colour-code each row: green for strong contenders, yellow for maybes, red for eliminated

## Open home checklist
- [ ] Arrive 5 minutes early to observe the queue, other buyers, and the agent's behaviour
- [ ] Take your own photos and video walkthrough: do not rely on listing photos
- [ ] Check for natural light in every room at the time of day you are visiting
- [ ] Test water pressure by running taps and flushing toilets
- [ ] Look for signs of damp: musty smells, bubbling paint, stained ceilings, mould in bathrooms
- [ ] Check power points, light switches and whether the property has sufficient circuits
- [ ] Inspect the roof from the street: look for sagging, missing tiles, or poor condition
- [ ] Check the condition of fences, gates, driveways, and outdoor structures
- [ ] Ask the agent about the property's history: previous sales, renovations, known issues
- [ ] Ask why the owners are selling and how long the property has been on the market
- [ ] Request a contract of sale and Section 10.7 certificate before you leave
- [ ] Note the advertised price method: fixed price, price guide, expressions of interest, or auction

## Preliminary due diligence
- [ ] Search the address on NSW's planning portal to check zoning and any nearby developments
- [ ] Check the flood, bushfire, and landslide risk maps for the property's location
- [ ] Search the address in the local council's DA tracker for recent or pending approvals nearby
- [ ] Google the street name plus "development" or "construction" to catch unadvertised projects
- [ ] Check if the property is in a heritage conservation area: this restricts renovations
- [ ] Search the EPA website for contaminated land registers near the property
- [ ] Check the NBN address lookup for connection type and estimated speeds
- [ ] Look up the property's sales history on CoreLogic or a free site like Property Value
