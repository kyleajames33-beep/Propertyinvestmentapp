# Offer & Negotiation Checklist: Experienced Investor

## Deal positioning
- [ ] Run updated comparables: sales in the last 90 days, adjusted for condition and market movement
- [ ] Confirm your numbers still work: yield, cash flow, renovation margin, or development feasibility
- [ ] Assess vendor motivation through the agent or your network: time pressure, portfolio liquidation, deceased estate
- [ ] Determine the property's days on market and any prior price reductions or failed campaigns

## Offer execution
- [ ] Submit a clean, terms-based offer: price, deposit, settlement, conditions: in writing
- [ ] Use settlement flexibility as a negotiating lever: 30 days for a keen vendor, 90 days if they need time
- [ ] Keep conditions minimal: finance and inspection only, no unnecessary clauses
- [ ] Set a 24-48 hour expiry to pressure a decision
- [ ] Anchor low but not insultingly: leave room for 2-3 counters before your ceiling

## Auction tactics
- [ ] Pre-register and inspect the contract at least 3 days prior
- [ ] Confirm your unconditional finance is in place and deposit funds are liquid
- [ ] Set your max bid and do not exceed it: one extra bid can destroy your margin
- [ ] If passed in, negotiate immediately using the pressure of a failed auction campaign

## Contract terms
- [ ] Include finance and inspection clauses with the shortest feasible timeframes
- [ ] Negotiate a 5% deposit if possible to preserve cash flow
- [ ] Align settlement with your portfolio cash flow: avoid month-end crunches
- [ ] Add any special conditions relevant to your strategy: early access for renovations, subject to DA
- [ ] Confirm no unfavourable special conditions inserted by the vendor
