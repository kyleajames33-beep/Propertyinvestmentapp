# Inspection & Due Diligence Checklist: Experienced Investor

## Structural and condition assessment
- [ ] Review building and pest inspection reports: focus on structural and safety issues, not cosmetics
- [ ] Estimate repair and renovation costs and confirm your margin still stacks up
- [ ] Check for asbestos, lead paint, or other hazardous materials if built pre-1990
- [ ] Assess the roof, foundation, and plumbing for any immediate capital requirements
- [ ] Verify the property's condition matches your strategy: turnkey, light renovation, or major rework

## Tenancy and income review
- [ ] Analyse the current lease: rent level, expiry, escalation clauses, and tenant quality
- [ ] Check rental payment history and any arrears or disputes on record
- [ ] Confirm the bond is correctly lodged and the condition report is complete
- [ ] Assess whether you can increase rent at renewal or if the tenant is paying above market
- [ ] Factor vacancy and re-leasing costs into your 12-month cash flow

## Strata and compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] Review the strata report: sinking fund, special levies, defects, and insurance adequacy
- [ ] Check for cladding, waterproofing, or structural defect risks in buildings under 10 years
- [ ] Confirm compliance with pool safety, smoke alarm, and electrical safety regulations
- [ ] Verify no outstanding council orders or enforcement actions

## Title and planning verification
- [ ] Confirm no easements, covenants, or caveats that restrict your intended use
- [ ] Check zoning and verify your plans: subdivision, development, change of use: are permissible
- [ ] Review nearby DAs that could impact value positively or negatively
- [ ] Verify flood, bushfire, and contamination risks and their insurance implications

## Portfolio impact final check
- [ ] Recalculate your total portfolio LVR post-acquisition
- [ ] Confirm the deal still meets your yield and growth thresholds after all costs
- [ ] Verify your cash flow model holds under stress: rate rise + vacancy scenario
- [ ] Lock in your maximum offer and walk-away point before entering negotiation
