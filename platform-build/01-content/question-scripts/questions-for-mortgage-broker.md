# Questions to Ask a Mortgage Broker

## Why These Questions Matter

A mortgage broker can save you time and money by accessing lenders and products you might not find on your own. But not all brokers are equal. Some specialise in first home buyers, others in investors or self-employed borrowers. Some have access to dozens of lenders, others work with a handful.

A good answer is specific, references recent experience, and includes real numbers. A vague answer about "helping lots of people" or "access to all major lenders" without detail should prompt follow-up questions. You are hiring this person to secure the best finance arrangement for your situation. Treat the conversation accordingly.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Licensing:** Verify the broker holds an Australian Credit Licence or is an authorised credit representative. Search the ASIC Connect Professional Registers.
- **Membership:** Look for membership with the Mortgage and Finance Association of Australia (MFAA) or the Finance Brokers Association of Australia (FBAA). These bodies require ongoing education and a code of practice.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews, ProductReview.com.au, and the AFCA (Australian Financial Complaints Authority) complaints register.
- **Lender panel:** Ask how many lenders they have on their panel before the meeting. If they say fewer than 20, that is worth questioning.

## Questions by Category

### Lender Panel and Product Breadth

1. How many lenders are on your panel, and can you name the three you placed the most business with in the last 12 months?
2. Of the loans you wrote last year, what percentage went to the top three lenders? What percentage went to lenders outside the big four?
3. Do you have access to non-bank lenders, credit unions, and building societies, or are you restricted to ADIs and major banks?

### Investment vs Owner-Occupier Experience

4. In the last 12 months, how many loans did you write for property investors versus owner-occupiers?
5. What loan structures do you typically recommend for investors who plan to hold properties long term, and what structures do you use for those focused on short-term cash flow?
6. Have you handled a loan for a buyer in my situation (e.g. first home buyer, investor with multiple properties, self-employed, trust structure) in the past six months? Walk me through that scenario.

### Commission Structure Transparency

7. How are you remunerated for the loan you will recommend to me? Is it a flat commission from the lender, a volume-based bonus, or a combination?
8. Are there any lenders on your panel that pay higher commissions than others? If so, how do you manage that conflict when recommending a loan?
9. Do you charge a direct fee to me as well as receiving commission from the lender? If so, under what circumstances?

### Pre-Approval Process

10. What type of pre-approval do you arrange: fully assessed or system-generated? What is the difference in reliability between the two?
11. How many of your pre-approvals in the last 12 months were honoured at the same terms when the buyer found a property? What caused the ones that changed to fall through?
12. What is the typical turnaround from submission to pre-approval with your preferred lenders at the moment?

### Ongoing Support

13. Once the loan settles, what ongoing support do you provide? Do you conduct annual rate reviews or check in only when I contact you?
14. If my circumstances change (job loss, inheritance, wanting to buy again), do I come back to you directly, or am I handed off to another team?
15. How do you handle situations where a lender changes rates or terms after settlement? Do you proactively contact clients about refinancing opportunities?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **"I have access to all lenders."** No broker has access to every lender in Australia. Some lenders do not work through brokers at all.
- **Pushes one lender heavily** without being able to explain why that lender is the best fit for your specific situation.
- **Cannot explain how they are paid** or becomes evasive when asked about commission and trail income.
- **Guarantees a specific rate or approval** before reviewing your full financial position. No broker can guarantee this.
- **Recommends interest-only loans for owner-occupiers** without a clear, documented reason tied to your strategy.
- **Pressure to sign documents immediately.** A competent broker gives you time to compare and decide.
- **No mention of comparison rates** when quoting interest rates. The advertised rate can be misleading without it.

---

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your total fee to me, if any, for this loan? Is it payable upfront, at settlement, or added to the loan?
- If I refinance through you in two years, do you charge a new fee?
- Are there any fees I will pay if I do not proceed with a loan after pre-approval?
- Do you receive trail commission from the lender after settlement? Does this influence which product you recommend?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Request a written comparison** of at least two loan options with comparison rates, fees, and features listed side by side.
2. **Verify the broker's licence** on the ASIC register if you have not already done so.
3. **Check the proposed lenders** on independent comparison sites to confirm the rates and features quoted.
4. **Do not sign anything** until you have reviewed the Credit Guide and Credit Proposal Disclosure documents. These are legal requirements and must be provided before you commit.
5. **Contact two other brokers** for comparison if you are not confident this broker is the right fit. The extra hour of your time is worth it on a decision this size.
