# Questions to Ask a Property Manager

## Why These Questions Matter

A property manager is responsible for one of your most significant assets. They find and screen tenants, handle maintenance, collect rent, conduct inspections, and ensure compliance with NSW tenancy laws. A capable manager protects your property, maximises your rental income, and reduces vacancy periods. A poor manager costs you money through extended vacancies, problem tenants, and neglected maintenance.

A good answer demonstrates thorough knowledge of the Residential Tenancies Act, references specific systems and processes, and provides clear data on vacancy rates and response times. You are engaging this manager to protect and grow the value of your investment. The interview is your opportunity to determine whether they are up to the task.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Licensing:** In NSW, property managers must work under the supervision of a licensed real estate agent. Verify the agency's licence and the individual manager's certificate of registration on the NSW Fair Trading licence check.
- **Portfolio size:** Ask how many properties the manager is currently responsible for. A common benchmark is 120-150 properties per manager. Above this, personal attention drops.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews and ask for references from landlords who own properties similar to yours in the same area.
- **Professional membership:** Look for membership with the Real Estate Institute of NSW (REINSW), which indicates ongoing professional development in tenancy law and property management best practice.

## Questions by Category

### Licensing

1. Who is the licensed real estate agent responsible for supervising your property management operations, and what is their licence number?
2. What is your certificate of registration number, and when did you last complete professional development training in property management?
3. How many properties are you personally managing right now, and what is the maximum number you believe you can manage while maintaining quality service?

### Fee Structure

4. What is your management fee as a percentage of the weekly rent, and is it charged on rent collected or rent payable? What does this fee include?
5. What is your letting fee, and is it charged as a percentage of the first week's rent or as a fixed amount? Is it payable every time a new tenant is found, or only at the start of the lease?
6. Are there additional fees for routine inspections, lease renewals, tribunal appearances, annual statements, or maintenance coordination? Can you provide a complete schedule of fees?
7. Do you charge a maintenance coordination fee or markup on tradesperson invoices? If so, what percentage?

### Communication and Reporting

8. How often do you conduct routine inspections, and what format is the report delivered in? Do you include photographs of the property condition?
9. What is your process for notifying me of maintenance issues? Do you contact me for approval on all repairs, or do you have an approval threshold?
10. How do I access my property's financial records and statements? Do you provide an online portal where I can view income, expenses, and invoices in real time?
11. What is your typical response time to landlord enquiries, and will I deal with you directly or with an assistant?

### Maintenance Approval Thresholds

12. What is your maintenance approval threshold, meaning the dollar amount below which you will authorise repairs without contacting me? How was this amount determined, and can I adjust it?
13. How do you select and vet the tradespeople you use? Do you obtain multiple quotes for larger repairs, and what dollar amount triggers that process?
14. If an urgent repair is required outside business hours, what is your after-hours process? Do you have an emergency contact line for both tenants and landlords?

### Tenant Screening Process

15. What is your tenant screening process? What checks do you conduct (credit history, rental references, employment verification, tribunal database), and what criteria disqualify an applicant?
16. How do you handle situations where multiple applications are received for the same property? What factors determine which tenant is approved?
17. What is your process for managing rent arrears? At what point do you contact the tenant, and at what point do you escalate to a termination notice or tribunal application?

### Vacancy Rate Performance

18. What was your portfolio's average vacancy rate over the last 12 months, and how does that compare to the suburb average?
19. What is your average time on market for properties similar to mine, and what strategies do you use to minimise vacancy periods between tenancies?

### Rent Review Approach

20. How do you determine the appropriate rent at the start of a tenancy, and how do you manage rent increases at lease renewal? Do you conduct a market review before recommending an increase?
21. If the market softens and I need to reduce the rent to retain a good tenant, what is your process for making that recommendation?

### Exit Terms

22. What is the notice period required to terminate your management agreement, and are there any exit fees or penalties?
23. If I want to switch to another agency, what is your process for transferring the tenancy file, bond records, and lease documentation? How long does the handover take?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **Each manager is responsible for more than 150 properties.** This makes personal attention and proactive management nearly impossible.
- **Cannot provide a complete schedule of fees** or mentions fees only when prompted. Hidden charges add up quickly.
- **No clear process for maintenance approvals** or tenant screening. Sloppy processes lead to expensive problems.
- **High vacancy rates compared to the suburb average.** This indicates poor marketing, tenant retention, or rent pricing.
- **No online portal or digital reporting system.** Inefficient systems cost you time and make it hard to track your investment.
- **Charges a letting fee every time a lease is renewed** rather than only at the start of a new tenancy. This creates a conflict of interest where the manager may prefer shorter tenancies.
- **Cannot explain the tenant screening criteria** or does not check tribunal databases. Problem tenants are the single biggest risk to your rental income.

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your total annual management cost for my property at the current market rent, including GST and all standard fees? Break this down item by item.
- Is the management fee calculated on gross rent collected or rent payable? If the tenant falls into arrears, do I still pay the management fee?
- Are there any fees charged when the property is vacant, such as an advertising fee or inspection fee?
- Do you receive referral fees or commissions from tradespeople you engage? If so, do you disclose these?
- What is the total cost to me if I terminate the agreement in the first six months versus after 12 months?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Request the management agreement** and review all fee schedules, termination clauses, and the agency's obligations. Have your solicitor review it if anything is unclear.
2. **Contact two current clients** of this manager. Ask about responsiveness, how maintenance is handled, and whether they would recommend the manager.
3. **Compare fee structures with two other agencies.** The cheapest is rarely the best, but large fee discrepancies deserve scrutiny.
4. **Confirm the manager's portfolio size** independently. Some agencies inflate or deflect when asked directly.
5. **Check the agency's tribunal record.** Frequent appearances at NCAT may indicate poor tenant screening or aggressive management practices.
