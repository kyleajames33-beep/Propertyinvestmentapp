# Shortlisting Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Investment scorecard
- [ ] Build a scorecard weighting yield potential, growth drivers, vacancy risk, and cash flow
- [ ] Set minimum thresholds for each score: any property below is automatically rejected
- [ ] Score properties out of 100 so you can rank your shortlist objectively
- [ ] Include a column for estimated renovation or improvement costs if value-add is part of your strategy

## Financial modelling
- [ ] Calculate gross rental yield: (weekly rent x 52) / purchase price x 100
- [ ] Estimate net yield by subtracting rates, insurance, management fees, maintenance, and vacancy allowance
- [ ] Model cash flow at current interest rates and at rates 2% higher
- [ ] Estimate annual depreciation using a rough guide: 2.5% of building value for capital works
- [ ] Include land tax in your cost model if buying in states where it applies to your situation
- [ ] Calculate your break-even vacancy period: how many weeks empty before you are out of pocket
- [ ] Build a simple 5-year projection showing rent growth, expense growth, and equity accumulation

## Property comparison
- [ ] Create a comparison table with purchase price, stamp duty, legals, and total acquisition cost
- [ ] Add ongoing costs: loan interest, rates, insurance, land tax, management, maintenance
- [ ] Include rental income, net yield, and cash flow position for each property
- [ ] Rank properties by your investment criteria score, not by how much you like them

## Open home and agent engagement
- [ ] Inspect the property with an investor lens: tenant appeal, not personal taste
- [ ] Assess whether the layout, condition, and features match what renters in the area want
- [ ] Ask the agent about current rental demand, typical tenant profiles, and average days to lease
- [ ] Request a rental estimate from the selling agent and independently verify it with a property manager
- [ ] Check if the property has any existing leases or is vacant: understand your immediate income position
- [ ] Photograph every room, the exterior, the street, and any maintenance issues for later review

## Preliminary due diligence
- [ ] Check zoning and nearby development applications on the council planning portal
- [ ] Review flood, bushfire, and coastal erosion risk maps for the location
- [ ] Search the address for past sales, price changes, and time on market
- [ ] Check if the property is in a strata or community title scheme and request the last AGM minutes
- [ ] Research the suburb's rental vacancy rate and time-on-market for rentals
- [ ] Confirm the property is not listed on any contaminated land or environmental hazard registers
