# Questions to Ask a Conveyancer

## Why These Questions Matter

A conveyancer handles the legal transfer of property ownership. In New South Wales, this means checking the contract, reviewing title documents, managing the settlement process through PEXA, and ensuring you do not inherit problems from the previous owner. A poor conveyancer can miss critical issues in a contract, cause settlement delays, or leave you unprotected if something goes wrong.

A good answer references specific NSW legislation, demonstrates recent hands-on experience with PEXA, and includes clear timelines. You are engaging this professional to protect your legal and financial interests in what is likely the largest transaction you will make. Do not treat this as a box-ticking exercise.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Licensing:** In NSW, conveyancers must hold a conveyancing licence issued by NSW Fair Trading. Verify the licence on the NSW Fair Trading licence check tool.
- **Professional association membership:** Look for membership with the Australian Institute of Conveyancers (NSW Division). This indicates ongoing professional development.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews and ask in local community groups about recent experiences. Settlement delays and poor communication are the most common complaints.
- **Insurance:** Confirm they hold professional indemnity insurance. This is a legal requirement in NSW but verify the coverage amount.

---

## Questions by Category

### NSW Licensing and Qualifications

1. Are you licensed by NSW Fair Trading to provide conveyancing services, and can you provide your licence number?
2. Are you a member of the Australian Institute of Conveyancers (NSW Division), and when did you last complete professional development?
3. Do you employ solicitors within your practice, or do you refer matters to an external solicitor when issues arise? If so, at what point does a matter get referred, and what does that cost me?

### Electronic Conveyancing (PEXA) Experience

4. How many settlements have you completed through PEXA in the last 12 months?
5. Have you handled a situation where PEXA was unavailable or a workspace failed on settlement day? What happened, and how was it resolved?
6. Do you verify the financial line items in the PEXA workspace yourself, or is that delegated to a junior staff member?

### Communication Style and Frequency

7. What is your standard process for keeping me updated? Do you send email updates at specific milestones, or do I need to chase you for information?
8. What is your typical response time to emails and phone calls during business hours? What about in the week leading up to settlement?
9. Will I deal with you directly throughout the transaction, or will my file be passed to different staff members at different stages?

### Fixed vs Hourly Fees

10. Is your fee fixed for a standard residential purchase, or do you charge hourly? What specific items are included in the fixed fee?
11. What services attract additional charges beyond your base fee? Examples might include contract review before exchange, Strata Plan inspection, or post-settlement follow-up.
12. If the settlement is delayed through no fault of mine, do you charge additional fees for the extension or revised settlement date?

### Contract Review Approach

13. When you review a contract for a buyer, what are the three most common issues you flag before exchange? Can you give me a recent example of a clause you negotiated that saved a buyer from a problem?
14. Do you review Section 10.7 certificates, strata reports, and building inspection reports as part of your standard service, or do you expect me to interpret those documents myself?

### Settlement Success Rate

15. In the last 12 months, what percentage of your settlements completed on the agreed date? Of those that did not, what were the typical causes?
16. Have you ever had a claim against your professional indemnity insurance related to a residential property transaction? If so, what was the nature of the issue and how was it resolved?

### Insurance

17. What is the amount of your professional indemnity insurance coverage, and does it cover both the purchase and the post-settlement period if an issue emerges later?
18. If an error on your part causes me financial loss, what is your process for managing that claim? Do you have a dispute resolution process before I need to go to NSW Civil and Administrative Tribunal?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **Cannot provide a NSW Fair Trading licence number** or the licence check shows disciplinary action.
- **Unfamiliar with PEXA** or has completed fewer than 20 electronic settlements. PEXA is now mandatory for most NSW property transactions.
- **Vague about fees** or provides a quote without specifying what is included and excluded.
- **Promises to review the contract in under 24 hours** but cannot explain what they actually check. Speed without thoroughness is dangerous.
- **No professional indemnity insurance** or coverage amount below $2 million for residential work.
- **You are passed between multiple staff members** during the first conversation. If this happens now, it will happen during your transaction.
- **Pressures you to exchange without proper due diligence.** A competent conveyancer will flag risks and give you time to decide.

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your total fee for a standard residential purchase in [suburb], including GST, from contract review through to settlement?
- Are searches and council fees charged separately, and can you provide an estimate of these third-party costs upfront?
- Do you charge for a pre-exchange contract review if I have not yet committed to using your services for the full transaction?
- Is there a cancellation fee if the purchase does not proceed after I have engaged you?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Verify the licence** on the NSW Fair Trading website using the licence number provided.
2. **Request a written fee proposal** that lists every item included in the fixed fee and every item that attracts an additional charge.
3. **Ask for references** from two buyers who completed a purchase in the last six months.
4. **Compare quotes from two other conveyancers.** Fees vary significantly, and the cheapest option is rarely the best protection.
5. **Confirm their availability** around your expected settlement date. Some conveyancers book out during peak periods.
