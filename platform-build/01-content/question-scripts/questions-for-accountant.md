# Questions to Ask an Accountant

## Why These Questions Matter

An accountant who understands property investment can save you thousands in tax, help you choose the right ownership structure, and keep you compliant with ATO requirements. A generalist accountant who prepares tax returns but does not understand negative gearing, capital gains tax, or trust structures can cost you deductions and expose you to compliance risks.

A good answer demonstrates specific experience with property investors, references recent ATO rulings, and offers proactive advice rather than just reactive compliance. You are hiring this accountant to optimise your tax position and ensure compliance across your property portfolio. Their expertise directly affects your after-tax returns.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Registration:** Verify the accountant is a registered tax agent with the Tax Practitioners Board (TPB). Search the TPB register online.
- **Professional membership:** Look for membership with CPA Australia, Chartered Accountants Australia and New Zealand (CA ANZ), or the Institute of Public Accountants (IPA). These bodies require ongoing education and adherence to professional standards.
- **Property specialisation:** Check their website and LinkedIn profile for evidence of property investment focus. Generalist firms may not have the depth you need.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews and ask for references from clients who have property portfolios similar to yours.

## Questions by Category

### Property Investment Specialisation

1. What percentage of your clients are property investors, and how many of those have portfolios of two or more investment properties?
2. In the last 12 months, how many tax returns have you prepared for clients who own investment properties? What is the average portfolio size of those clients?
3. Do you invest in property yourself? While not essential, an accountant who understands property from personal experience may offer more practical advice.

### Structure Advice Capability

4. What ownership structures do you typically recommend for property investors, and can you explain the tax implications of each (individual, joint tenants, tenants in common, discretionary trust, unit trust, SMSF)?
5. For a buyer in my situation, what structure would you recommend, and what are the specific advantages and disadvantages over holding the property in my personal name?
6. Do you advise on setting up trusts and SMSFs, or do you refer that work to a specialist? If you refer it, who do you work with, and what is the additional cost?
7. What are the land tax implications of the structure you are recommending in NSW, and how does that change as my portfolio grows?

### Negative Gearing and CGT Knowledge

8. How do you ensure your clients maximise deductible expenses related to their investment properties while remaining compliant with ATO requirements? Can you give me two examples of deductions clients often miss?
9. What is your approach to depreciation schedules? Do you work with a specific quantity surveyor, or do I need to engage one independently?
10. When I eventually sell the property, what will my CGT liability look like under the structure you have recommended? How do you model this for clients during their holding period?
11. How do you handle the six-year absence rule and other CGT exemptions for clients who have lived in a property before renting it out?

### ATO Dispute Experience

12. Have you represented clients in ATO audits or disputes related to investment property deductions? What was the outcome, and how was the process managed?
13. If the ATO queries a deduction I have claimed, what support do you provide? Do you manage the correspondence and negotiations, or do I need to engage a tax lawyer?
14. How do you keep up with changes in tax legislation and ATO rulings that affect property investors? Can you reference a recent change and how it affected your clients?

### Fee Structure

15. How do you structure your fees? Is it a fixed annual fee, hourly billing, or a combination? What is included in the base fee?
16. For a client with my property ownership structure and portfolio size, what was your total fee last year to similar clients? Give me a range if you cannot provide an exact figure.
17. Are there additional charges for advice outside of tax return preparation, such as structuring advice, ATO correspondence, or estate planning considerations?

### Proactive Advice vs Reactive Compliance

18. Beyond preparing my tax return, what proactive advice do you provide throughout the year? Do you contact me when legislative changes affect my position, or only when I contact you?
19. Do you conduct an annual review of my portfolio to identify opportunities for restructuring, additional deductions, or risk mitigation?
20. If I am considering a new purchase, do you provide pre-purchase advice on the tax implications of that acquisition, or is your involvement limited to post-purchase compliance?

### Record-Keeping Systems

21. What record-keeping system do you recommend for property investors? Do you have a preferred software platform (e.g. Xero, MYOB, a property-specific app), or do you accept manual records?
22. What documentation do you require from me throughout the year to ensure my tax return is accurate and defensible? How do you want that information delivered?
23. If I am missing a receipt or invoice, what is your policy? Do you accept bank statements as evidence, or do you require original documentation for all claims?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **The majority of their clients are not property investors.** You need someone who understands property tax week in, week out.
- **Cannot explain the tax implications** of different ownership structures with specific numbers relevant to your situation.
- **Unfamiliar with recent ATO rulings** or legislative changes affecting property investors. This indicates they are not keeping current.
- **No experience with ATO audits or disputes.** If the ATO comes knocking, you want an accountant who has been through the process before.
- **Provides advice that sounds like a tax scheme** or promises deductions that seem too good to be true. Aggressive positions increase your audit risk.
- **Only reactive engagement.** An accountant who only sees you once a year at tax time is missing opportunities to optimise your position.
- **Unwilling to put advice in writing.** Verbal advice is difficult to rely on if questioned by the ATO.

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your total annual fee for a client with my portfolio structure, including tax return preparation and any standard advice?
- Are there additional charges for phone calls, emails, or meetings outside the tax return process? How are these billed?
- If I engage you for pre-purchase advice on a specific property, what is the cost, and what does the advice cover?
- What is the cost if I am audited by the ATO and need your support through the process? Is this covered by my annual fee or charged separately?
- Do you offer a service package for property investors that includes quarterly reviews, tax planning, and the annual return?

---

## After the Meeting

1. **Verify the tax agent registration** on the Tax Practitioners Board website. Check for any disciplinary actions or conditions on their registration.
2. **Request a written fee proposal** that specifies what is included in the annual fee and what attracts additional charges.
3. **Ask for two references** from property investor clients with portfolios similar to yours. Contact them and ask about the quality of advice and responsiveness.
4. **Confirm they have professional indemnity insurance.** This protects you if incorrect advice causes a financial loss.
5. **Compare with one other property-specialist accountant.** Fee structures and service models vary, and the right fit depends on how hands-on you want them to be.
