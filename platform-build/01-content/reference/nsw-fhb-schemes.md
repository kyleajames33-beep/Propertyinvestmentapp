# NSW First Home Buyer Schemes: Complete Guide

> Last updated: July 2025. This guide covers all state and federal schemes available to first home buyers in NSW. Educational content only, not financial advice.

---

## Overview of available schemes

First home buyers in NSW currently have access to multiple assistance programs at both state and federal level. This guide covers each scheme, its eligibility criteria, application process, and how schemes can be combined.

| Scheme | Type | Level | New or existing home? |
|---|---|---|---|
| First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme (FHBAS) | Stamp duty exemption/concession | State | Both |
| First Home Owner Grant (New Homes) | $10,000 cash grant | State | New homes only |
| First Home Buyer Choice | Annual property tax (closed to new entrants) | State | Both (grandfathered only) |
| Shared Equity Home Buyer Helper | Government equity contribution | State | Both |
| First Home Super Saver Scheme | Superannuation withdrawal | Federal | Both |
| Help to Buy | Federal shared equity | Federal | Both |
| Home Guarantee Scheme (5% Deposit) | LMI waiver | Federal | Both |

## 1. First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme (FHBAS)

### What it is

FHBAS provides a full or partial exemption from transfer duty (stamp duty) for eligible first home buyers purchasing property valued up to $1 million [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Exemption and concession thresholds

| Property type | Full exemption | Partial concession | No concession |
|---|---|---|---|
| New or existing home | Up to $800,000 | $800,001 to $1,000,000 | Over $1,000,000 |
| Vacant land | Up to $350,000 | $350,001 to $450,000 | Over $450,000 |

A full exemption on an $800,000 property saves approximately $31,090 in transfer duty [Source: Revenue NSW FHBAS Calculator, 2025].

### Eligibility checklist

To qualify for FHBAS, you must [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]:

- Be 18 years or older.
- Be an Australian citizen or permanent resident (at least one applicant).
- Have never owned or co-owned residential property in Australia (limited exceptions for property owned before 1 July 2000).
- Be purchasing as a natural person (not a company or trust).
- Intend to occupy the property as your principal place of residence.
- Move into the property within 12 months of settlement (or completion for new builds) and live there continuously for at least 12 months (6 months for contracts exchanged before 1 July 2023).

### Application process

1. Confirm eligibility against the criteria above.
2. Complete the First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme application form.
3. Complete the Purchaser/Transferee Declaration form.
4. Lodge both forms with Revenue NSW via your solicitor or conveyancer at settlement [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Common traps

- If you or your spouse/partner has owned property anywhere in Australia (not just NSW), you are generally ineligible.
- The 12-month continuous residence requirement is strict. If you move out early, you may have to repay the exemption.
- Both partners in a couple are assessed. If one has owned property, the couple is generally ineligible.
- If purchasing through a discretionary trust, FHBAS is not available.

## 2. First Home Owner Grant (New Homes)

### What it is

A one-off $10,000 grant for eligible first home buyers who purchase or build a new home that has not been previously occupied or sold as a residence [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Property value caps

| Property type | Value cap |
|---|---|
| New home, apartment, or substantially renovated property | Purchase price must not exceed $600,000 |
| House and land package or building from scratch | Combined land and build value must not exceed $750,000 |

[Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]

### What counts as a new home

A "new home" means a dwelling that [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]:

- Has not been previously occupied or sold as a place of residence.
- Is substantially renovated and has not been occupied since the renovation.
- Is purchased off-the-plan (provided it is a new dwelling).
- Is built to replace a demolished dwelling on the same land.

### Eligibility checklist

- All applicants must be at least 18 years old.
- At least one applicant must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident.
- Neither you nor your spouse/partner can have previously owned residential property in Australia (with limited exceptions).
- Neither you nor your spouse/partner can have previously received a First Home Owner Grant in any Australian state or territory.
- You must apply as a natural person (not a company or trust).
- You must occupy the property as your principal place of residence for at least 6 continuous months (12 months for contracts signed after July 2023), commencing within 12 months of settlement or build completion [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Application process

- Apply through your bank or financial institution when arranging finance (most common method).
- Alternatively, apply directly to Revenue NSW.
- The grant is paid at settlement for completed homes, or at the first drawdown for construction loans [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Common traps

- The grant is strictly for new homes only. Existing (established) properties do not qualify.
- The $600,000 / $750,000 caps are firm. Even $1 over disqualifies you.
- Substantially renovated homes must not have been occupied since renovation. Check with the vendor.

## 3. First Home Buyer Choice (Property Tax vs Stamp Duty)

### What it was

First Home Buyer Choice was a NSW Government scheme that operated from 16 January 2023 to 30 June 2023. It allowed eligible first home buyers to choose between paying upfront stamp duty or an annual property tax [Source: NSW Government, 2022].

### How it worked

Participants who chose property tax paid [Source: NSW Government, 2022]:

- An annual property tax of $400 plus 0.3% of the land value (for owner-occupiers), OR
- $1,500 plus 1.1% of the land value (for investors).

### Current status

- The scheme is **closed to new entrants** as of 1 July 2023.
- Existing participants who opted in remain grandfathered and continue paying annual property tax for as long as they own the property [Source: NSW Government, 2023].
- If a grandfathered property is sold, the property tax obligation transfers to the new owner [Source: Revenue NSW].

### Long-term cost comparison

Whether property tax or stamp duty was cheaper depends on how long the buyer owned the property and how land values changed. For short-term ownership (under 5-7 years), property tax was typically cheaper. For long-term ownership, upfront stamp duty was usually more economical. Buyers of grandfathered properties should check whether property tax applies before purchasing [Source: Revenue NSW].

## 4. Shared Equity Home Buyer Helper (NSW)

### What it is

A NSW Government pilot program where the government contributes up to 40% of the purchase price of a new home or 30% of an existing home in exchange for an equivalent equity share. The buyer needs only a 2% deposit and takes out a mortgage for the remaining portion [Source: Revenue NSW; LAHC, 2024].

### Eligibility criteria

| Element | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Income (singles) | $93,200 or less gross annual income |
| Income (couples) | $124,200 or less combined gross annual income |
| Property price (Sydney and major regional centres) | Up to $950,000 |
| Property price (other regional areas) | Up to $600,000 |
| Minimum deposit | 2% of purchase price |

[Source: Revenue NSW Shared Equity Customer Guide, 2024; NSW Parliament, 2024]

### Eligible cohorts

The scheme is restricted to [Source: Revenue NSW, 2024]:

- Single parents with a non-adult child or children.
- Single people aged 50 years or over.
- First home buyers who are key workers (nurse, midwife, teacher, early childhood educator, paramedic, or police officer).
- Victim-survivors of domestic and family violence (since December 2023).

### How the equity share works

- The government's equity contribution reduces the amount the buyer needs to borrow.
- The buyer does not pay rent or interest on the government's share.
- When the property is sold, the government receives its equity percentage of the sale proceeds.
- The buyer can make voluntary payments to buy out the government's share over time [Source: Revenue NSW, 2024].

### Repayment terms

- Buyers can remain in the scheme as long as they continue to meet eligibility requirements.
- Periodic reviews are conducted (first review 2 years after settlement, then every 2 years, extendable to 5 years).
- If a review finds the buyer is no longer eligible, they may be required to buy out the government's equity if they have the capacity to do so. Buyers are never forced to sell their property [Source: Revenue NSW Shared Equity Customer Guide, 2024].

### Application process

1. Confirm eligibility (income, assets, eligible cohort).
2. Apply through an approved panel lender.
3. Obtain pre-approval for a standard home loan.
4. Apply for Shared Equity approval through Revenue NSW.
5. Sign the Shared Equity Agreement.
6. Complete the property purchase.

[Source: Revenue NSW, 2024]

### Asset limits

Financial assets must not exceed [Source: Revenue NSW, 2024]:

| Applicant type | Asset limit |
|---|---|
| Joint applicants with income over $93,200 | 30% of purchase price |
| Single applicants aged 18-49; joint applicants with income up to $93,200 | 45% of purchase price |
| Single applicants aged 50+; DFV victim-survivors | 65% of purchase price |

## 5. First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS) - Federal

### What it is

A federal scheme that allows first home buyers to make voluntary contributions to their superannuation fund and later withdraw those contributions (plus associated earnings) to help purchase a first home [Source: ATO, 2025].

### Contribution limits

| Limit | Amount |
|---|---|
| Maximum per financial year | $15,000 |
| Maximum total across all years | $50,000 per person ($100,000 for couples) |

[Source: ATO, 2025]

### How it works

- You can contribute voluntarily to super via salary sacrifice (concessional) or after-tax personal contributions (non-concessional).
- When ready to buy, you request a FHSS determination from the ATO to find out how much you can withdraw.
- You can withdraw: 100% of non-concessional contributions; 85% of concessional contributions; plus associated earnings calculated by the ATO [Source: ATO, 2025].
- Tax is withheld at your marginal rate less a 30% offset on the assessable amount.

### Withdrawal process

1. Request a FHSS determination via myGov/ATO online services BEFORE signing a contract.
2. Review the determination (shows maximum withdrawal amount).
3. Submit a release request.
4. Receive funds within 15-25 business days.
5. You must sign a contract to purchase or start construction within 12 months of release (or request an extension) [Source: ATO, 2025].

### Tax treatment

- Concessional contributions going into super are taxed at 15% (instead of your marginal rate).
- On withdrawal, the assessable amount is taxed at your marginal rate less a 30% offset.
- For most people on a 32.5% marginal rate, the effective tax on withdrawal is approximately 2.5%.
- This creates a net tax saving compared to saving outside super [Source: ATO, 2025].

### Eligibility checklist

- Be 18 years or older when requesting release.
- Have never owned property in Australia (including investment, vacant land, or commercial property).
- Have never made a previous FHSS release request.
- Intend to occupy the property as your principal place of residence for at least 6 months within the first 12 months.
- Contributions must be voluntary (employer Super Guarantee contributions do not count) [Source: ATO, 2025].

### Common traps

- You MUST request a determination BEFORE signing a contract. Doing so after is too late.
- Employer Super Guarantee contributions cannot be withdrawn.
- The 12-month timeframe to purchase is strict. Extensions may be granted.
- If you don't buy a property, you must recontribute the released amount to super or pay FHSS tax.
- Associated earnings are calculated by the ATO using a statutory rate, not your actual fund returns.

## 6. Help to Buy Scheme - Federal

### What it is

A federal shared equity scheme that helps eligible home buyers purchase a home with a minimum 2% deposit. The Australian Government contributes up to 40% of the purchase price for a new home or 30% for an existing home, in exchange for an equivalent equity interest [Source: Australian Government, 2025].

### Current status

Applications opened on 5 December 2025 [Source: Australian Government; Housing Australia, 2025].

### Eligibility criteria

| Element | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Individual income | $100,000 or less |
| Joint/single parent income | $160,000 or less |
| Minimum deposit | 2% of purchase price |
| Citizenship | Australian citizens aged 18+ |
| Property ownership | Must not currently own property (limited exceptions for single parents) |

[Source: Australian Government Help to Buy, 2025]

### Property price caps

| Location (NSW) | Price cap |
|---|---|
| Sydney and regional centres (Newcastle, Central Coast, Illawarra) | $1,300,000 |
| Rest of NSW | $800,000 |

[Source: Australian Government Help to Buy, 2025]

### Participating states

Available in NSW, Victoria, Queensland, South Australia, ACT, and Northern Territory from 5 December 2025. Western Australia is expected to join in early-to-mid 2026. Tasmania has decided not to participate at this stage [Source: Australian Government, 2025].

### Participating lenders

Commonwealth Bank of Australia and Bank Australia are the initial participating lenders, with more expected to join in 2026 [Source: Australian Government, 2025].

### How it works

- The government takes an equity share (up to 40% new / 30% existing) via a second mortgage.
- The buyer obtains a first mortgage from a participating lender for the remaining amount.
- The buyer does not pay rent or interest on the government's share.
- Buyers can make voluntary repayments to buy out the government's share.
- When the property is sold, the government receives its equity percentage of proceeds [Source: Australian Government, 2025].

### Application process

1. Check eligibility with a participating lender.
2. Gather documents (Notice of Assessment, bank statements, identification, asset/liability details).
3. Apply through a participating lender (not directly to Housing Australia).
4. Obtain pre-approval from both the lender and Housing Australia.
5. Find a property within the price cap.
6. Complete the purchase [Source: Australian Government, 2025].

## 7. Home Guarantee Scheme (5% Deposit Scheme) - Federal

### What it is

A federal scheme that allows eligible first home buyers to purchase a home with a deposit as low as 5% without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). The government provides a guarantee to the lender for up to 15% of the property value [Source: Housing Australia, 2025].

### Key changes from 1 October 2025

- **Uncapped places**: All eligible first home buyers can apply (no annual limit).
- **No income caps**: Previously $125,000 for individuals and $200,000 for couples.
- **Higher property price caps**: Increased to reflect market conditions.
- **Simpler regional access**: Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee merged into the First Home Guarantee [Source: Housing Australia, 2025].

### Property price caps (from 1 October 2025)

| Location | Cap |
|---|---|
| NSW - capital city and regional centre | $1,500,000 |
| NSW - other | $800,000 |

[Source: Housing Australia, 2025]

### Eligibility

- First home buyers who have not owned property in Australia in the past 10 years.
- Must occupy the property as principal place of residence.
- Must have saved at least 5% but less than 20% deposit.
- Available through over 30 participating lenders [Source: Housing Australia, 2025].

## Which schemes can be combined

### Schemes that can be stacked (used together)

| Combination | Allowed? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FHBAS + FHOG | Yes | Common combination for new homes under $600,000 |
| FHBAS + FHSSS | Yes | FHSSS for deposit, FHBAS for stamp duty relief |
| FHOG + FHSSS | Yes | For new homes |
| FHBAS + Home Guarantee Scheme | Yes | Stamp duty relief plus LMI waiver |
| Shared Equity + FHSSS | Check | FHSSS can fund the 2% deposit |

### Schemes that are mutually exclusive

| Combination | Allowed? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Help to Buy + Home Guarantee Scheme | No | Help to Buy explicitly excludes participants in other government guarantee schemes |
| Shared Equity (NSW) + Help to Buy | No | Cannot receive both state and federal shared equity for the same purchase |
| FHBAS + full stamp duty exemption + First Home Buyer Choice | N/A | First Home Buyer Choice is closed to new entrants |

## Summary: which scheme for which persona?

| Persona | Recommended schemes |
|---|---|
| FHB-OO buying existing home under $800k | FHBAS (full stamp duty exemption) + FHSSS + Home Guarantee Scheme |
| FHB-OO buying existing home $800k-$1M | FHBAS (concession) + FHSSS |
| FHB-OO buying new home under $600k | FHBAS + FHOG ($10,000) + FHSSS + Home Guarantee Scheme |
| FHB-OO key worker / single parent / 50+ | Consider Shared Equity Home Buyer Helper |
| Any FHB with small deposit (5%) | Home Guarantee Scheme (LMI waiver) |
| Any FHB saving for deposit | FHSSS (tax savings on contributions) |

## Conflicting information noted

- **FHOG residence period**: Some sources cite 6 months, others cite 12 months. The 12-month requirement applies to contracts signed after July 2023. The 6-month requirement applies to earlier contracts. Current contracts require 12 months [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].
- **First Home Buyer Choice status**: Some older sources still list this as an active option. It was closed to new entrants on 1 July 2023. Only grandfathered participants remain in the scheme.
- **FHBAS maximum savings**: Sources vary between "$21,529" and "$31,090." The correct figure depends on the property value and the date of the calculation. For a property at exactly $800,000 under current rates, the maximum saving is approximately $31,090.
- **Help to Buy launch date**: Early sources indicated "late 2024." The scheme actually opened for applications on 5 December 2025.
- **Shared Equity income thresholds**: Some sources cite slightly different figures ($90,000/$120,000 in older material vs $93,200/$124,200 in the current Revenue NSW guide). The higher figures reflect annual indexation.

## 2024/2025 threshold updates confirmed

- FHBAS property value thresholds remain at $800,000 (full exemption) and $1,000,000 (concession) since 1 July 2023 [Source: Revenue NSW, 2023].
- Shared Equity income thresholds indexed annually to NSW Wage Price Index [Source: Revenue NSW, 2024].
- Help to Buy income caps raised from $90,000/$120,000 to $100,000/$160,000 in the 2025 Federal Budget [Source: Federal Treasury, 2025].
- Home Guarantee Scheme expanded: uncapped places, no income caps, higher price caps from 1 October 2025 [Source: Housing Australia, 2025].
- NSW property price cap for Help to Buy set at $1,300,000 (Sydney/regional centres) and $800,000 (rest of NSW) [Source: Australian Government, 2025].
