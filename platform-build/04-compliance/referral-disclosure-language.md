# Referral arrangement disclosure language

## Purpose

This document provides comprehensive disclosure language for the platform's referral arrangements with professional service providers. It addresses requirements under:

- ASIC Regulatory Guide 203 *Do I need a credit licence?* (RG 203) for credit referral disclosure
- The National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009 (Cth) (NCCP Act) and National Credit Regulations for referral exemptions
- The Australian Consumer Law (ACL) for disclosure of commercial relationships
- ASIC guidance on avoiding misleading or deceptive conduct in referral arrangements

Under the downstream referral exemption in reg 25(2) and reg 25(2A) of the National Credit Regulations, a referrer must disclose to the consumer any benefits received for giving the referral, in the same form as the referral information. See RG 203.119.

Under the ACL, failing to disclose a commercial relationship (such as a referral fee arrangement) may constitute misleading or deceptive conduct under s18 of the ACL if the overall impression conveyed is that a recommendation is independent when it is not. See ACCC guidance on misleading and deceptive conduct and the AANA Code of Ethics on transparency about commercial relationships.

## Regulatory framework

### ASIC requirements for referral arrangements

#### Credit activity licensing

Under the NCCP Act, a person must not engage in credit activities unless they hold an Australian Credit Licence (ACL) or are exempt. Credit activities include:

- providing credit assistance (s8): suggesting a consumer apply for a particular credit contract with a particular credit provider, or assisting a consumer to apply
- acting as an intermediary (s9): acting between a credit provider and a consumer to secure provision of credit

See NCCP Act ss6-9 and ASIC RG 203.

#### The mere referral exemption

A person who merely refers a consumer to a licensed credit provider or representative may be exempt from the licensing requirement if they meet the conditions in the National Credit Regulations. There are two types:

**Downstream referrals** (reg 25(2) and reg 25(2A)):
- You inform the consumer that a licensee or representative can provide particular credit activities
- You give the consumer information about how to contact the licensee or representative (for example, a link from a website)
- You disclose to the consumer any benefits (such as commissions) you may receive for giving the referral
- The disclosure must be in the same form as the information about how to contact the licensee. See RG 203.119.

**Upstream referrals** (reg 25(5)):
- You give a licensee or representative the consumer's name, contact details and a description of their credit requirements
- You must have a written referral agreement with the licensee
- The referral must be incidental to carrying on another business
- You must obtain the consumer's consent to pass on their details
- You must pass on the details within 5 business days
- You must disclose to the consumer any benefits you may receive for the referral
- You must not require the consumer to pay a fee for the referral
- You must not conduct the referral business from non-standard premises (for example, temporary stalls)

See RG 203.115-RG 203.121.

#### Excluded benefits under RG 246

The ban on conflicted remuneration under Division 4 of Part 7.7A of the Corporations Act applies to benefits that could reasonably be expected to influence the advice given. The ban does not apply to benefits that are excluded from the definition of conflicted remuneration, such as benefits paid by the client (s963A(1)(b)). See ASIC RG 246 *Conflicted and other banned remuneration*.

Referral fees paid by a third party (not the client) may be conflicted remuneration if they could reasonably be expected to influence advice. However, where the platform provides only general information and referrals (not financial product advice), the conflicted remuneration ban in its strict sense may not apply. The platform must still ensure that referral arrangements do not create conflicts that could lead to misleading or deceptive conduct under the ACL. See RG 246.4-RG 246.6.

### ACCC requirements for disclosure of commercial relationships

#### Section 18 of the ACL

Section 18 of the ACL prohibits conduct in trade or commerce that is misleading or deceptive, or likely to mislead or deceive. Silence or omission can constitute misleading conduct if disclosure of a material fact would be expected. See ACCC v TPG Internet Pty Ltd [2013] HCA 54 and ACCC v Employsure (Federal Court).

If the platform presents a referral partner's service as a recommendation without disclosing that a commercial payment is received for the referral, a consumer may reasonably believe the recommendation is independent and impartial. This may constitute misleading or deceptive conduct under s18 of the ACL.

#### AANA Code of Ethics

The AANA Code of Ethics requires that advertising and marketing communications must be clearly distinguishable as such. Where an influencer, affiliate or referral platform accepts payment in exchange for promoting a brand's products or services, the relationship must be clear, obvious and upfront. Labels such as "#ad", "Advertising", "Paid Partnership" or "Paid Promotion" are considered sufficient. Less clear labels such as "#sp", "Spon", "gifted", "Affiliate", "Collab" or merely mentioning the brand name may not be sufficient. See the AANA Code of Ethics and Practice Note.

## Disclosure statement templates

### Short disclosure (20 words) - CTA buttons and inline links

> We may receive a referral fee if you choose to contact this professional. You are free to choose any provider.

**Placement:** Immediately adjacent to every "Contact", "Get in touch" or "Request referral" button or link. Must be visible before the user clicks.

### Medium disclosure (50 words) - referral cards and partner listings

> [Platform Name] receives a referral fee when you engage with professionals through our platform. Our partners are selected based on criteria including licensing, experience and customer feedback, but they do not influence our editorial content. You are under no obligation to use any referred professional and may choose any provider you prefer.

**Placement:** Below each partner profile card, in referral request confirmation emails, on partner directory pages.

### Long disclosure (150 words) - dedicated disclosure page, footer link

> [Platform Name] operates a referral service that connects users with professional service providers in the property and finance sectors. When you choose to engage with a professional through our platform, we may receive a referral fee, commission or other benefit from that professional or their organisation. The amount and structure of these fees varies by partner and may be a fixed amount, a percentage of fees earned, or a combination.
>
> Our referral partners include mortgage brokers, conveyancers, property inspectors, buyer's agents, financial advisers and other property professionals. Partners are selected based on criteria including appropriate licensing, industry experience and customer feedback. However, the existence of a referral arrangement does not constitute an endorsement or recommendation of that partner's services.
>
> Our editorial content is prepared independently and is not influenced by our referral partners. Referral partners do not review or approve our content before publication. You are under no obligation to use any professional referred through our platform and may engage any provider of your choice.

**Placement:** Dedicated /referral-disclosure page linked from the website footer sitewide and from every referral CTA.

## Referral fee structure disclosure

### How referral fees work

The platform must provide a clear description of how referral fees are structured. The following is a template:

> Referral fees may be structured in one or more of the following ways:
>
> - **Fixed fee:** a set dollar amount paid for each successful referral, regardless of the value of the transaction
> - **Percentage fee:** a percentage of the professional's fee or commission earned from your engagement
> - **Hybrid model:** a combination of fixed and percentage components
> - **Ongoing commission:** in some cases, a small ongoing payment if you continue to use the professional's services
>
> The specific fee arrangement with each partner is confidential and varies depending on the type of service. If you would like more information about referral fees for a specific partner, please contact us.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] The specific fee structures and amounts must be verified against actual referral agreements. The description must be accurate; a misleading description of fee arrangements would itself be a breach of s18 of the ACL.

## Partner selection and independence disclosure

### How partners are selected

> We select referral partners based on the following criteria:
>
> - holding all required licences and professional memberships (for example, Australian Credit Licence, AFS licence, conveyancing licence, real estate licence)
> - demonstrated experience and track record in their field
> - positive customer feedback and complaint history
> - commitment to professional standards and customer service
>
> While we have commercial arrangements with our referral partners, these arrangements do not influence our editorial content. Our articles, guides and tools are prepared independently by our editorial team. Referral partners do not review, edit or approve our content before publication.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] The partner selection criteria must be verified against actual vetting processes. If the platform does not in fact vet partners against these criteria, the statement must be amended or removed to avoid misleading conduct under the ACL.

## User choice disclosure

### Referral is optional

> Using a professional referred through [Platform Name] is entirely your choice. You are not obligated to use any referred professional. You may:
>
> - choose your own professional service provider independently
> - compare services and fees from multiple providers before making a decision
> - negotiate fees and terms directly with any professional
> - decline a referral at any time, including after initial contact
>
> Your decision to use or not use a referred professional will not affect your ability to use the other features of our platform.

## Specific disclosure by professional type

### Mortgage brokers

> [Platform Name] does not hold an Australian Credit Licence and does not provide credit assistance. Any referral to a mortgage broker is a mere referral only under reg 25(2) of the National Credit Regulations. The mortgage broker referred to you holds their own ACL or is a credit representative of an ACL holder. They are responsible for all credit assistance, responsible lending assessments and disclosure requirements under the NCCP Act. We may receive a referral fee if you engage with the mortgage broker through our platform.

### Conveyancers and solicitors

> We may refer you to a conveyancer or solicitor for property transaction services. Conveyancers and solicitors are regulated by their respective state and territory law societies and professional bodies. They are responsible for their own professional advice, fees and service standards. We may receive a referral fee if you engage their services through our platform.

### Property inspectors and building certifiers

> We may refer you to property inspectors, building certifiers or pest inspectors. These professionals are responsible for their own assessments, reports and opinions. We do not warrant the quality or accuracy of any inspection report. We may receive a referral fee if you engage their services through our platform.

### Buyer's agents

> We may refer you to a buyer's agent or property advocate. Buyer's agents are typically licensed real estate agents and are regulated by the relevant state or territory authority. They act on your behalf in property negotiations and transactions. We may receive a referral fee if you engage their services through our platform.

### Financial advisers

> Any financial adviser referred through our platform holds their own Australian Financial Services Licence or is an authorised representative of an AFS licensee. They are responsible for any financial product advice they provide. [Platform Name] does not provide financial product advice. We may receive a referral fee if you engage with a financial adviser through our platform. The adviser will provide you with a Financial Services Guide and Statement of Advice as required by the Corporations Act.

### Real estate agents

> We may refer you to real estate agents for property sales or management services. Real estate agents are licensed and regulated by the relevant state or territory authority. They are responsible for their own services, fees and professional obligations. We may receive a referral fee if you engage their services through our platform.

## Required disclosure placement

### Mandatory placement locations

| Location | Disclosure required | Form |
|---|---|---|
| Website footer sitewide | Link to dedicated disclosure page | Text link, visible on every page |
| Every CTA for referral | Short disclosure (20 words) | Inline text immediately adjacent to button or link |
| Partner profile pages | Medium disclosure (50 words) | Below partner description, above contact form |
| Referral request confirmation | Medium disclosure (50 words) | In confirmation email and on-screen message |
| Dedicated disclosure page | Long disclosure (150 words) plus fee structure | Full page at /referral-disclosure |
| Before referral form submission | Medium or long disclosure | Above the submit button on referral request forms |
| Mortgage-related content | AFSL/ACL disclaimer plus referral disclosure | Combined with financial product advice disclaimer |

### Proximity requirement

Under RG 203.119, the disclosure of referral benefits must be "in the same form as the information about how to contact the licensee or representative." This means:

- if contact information is given in writing, the commission disclosure must also be in writing
- if contact information is given orally, the disclosure must be given orally
- if contact information is provided via a link, the disclosure must be provided alongside that link

For the platform, this means the disclosure must appear in the same visual area as the referral CTA. A link to a separate disclosure page in the website footer is not sufficient on its own. The short disclosure must appear at the point of referral.

## Comparison service disclosure

### If the platform operates as a comparison service

If the platform allows users to compare multiple professionals side by side, the following additional disclosure applies:

> [Platform Name] provides information about professional service providers to help you compare your options. Not all providers in the market are included in our comparison. The order in which professionals are displayed may be influenced by commercial arrangements, including referral fees. Professionals with whom we have referral arrangements may be displayed more prominently. You should always compare services from multiple sources before making a decision.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] This disclosure is only required if the platform operates as a comparison service. If professionals are displayed in a ranked or ordered format, the basis of the ranking must be disclosed. A failure to disclose that rankings are influenced by commercial payments may constitute misleading or deceptive conduct under s18 of the ACL. See ACCC v TPG Internet Pty Ltd on the prominence of qualifying information.

## The platform does not hold an Australian Credit Licence

### Implications for mortgage broker referrals

[Platform Name] does not hold an Australian Credit Licence (ACL). This has specific implications for mortgage broker referrals:

1. The platform cannot provide credit assistance. It can only make mere referrals under the exemption in reg 25(2) of the National Credit Regulations.

2. The platform must not:
   - suggest a consumer apply for a particular credit contract with a particular credit provider (this would be credit assistance under s8 of the NCCP Act)
   - assist a consumer to apply for credit (for example, by explaining application forms or compiling information)
   - negotiate terms of a credit contract on behalf of a consumer
   - receive application documents or payments to pass to a credit provider

   See RG 203.68-RG 203.70.

3. The downstream referral exemption (reg 25(2)) only applies if the referral is limited to informing the consumer that a licensee can provide credit activities and giving contact information. If the platform does more than this (for example, recommending a specific lender or loan product), the exemption is lost and the platform would be engaging in unlicensed credit activity. See RG 203.117.

4. The platform must disclose any referral fee to the consumer at the time of referral, in the same form as the referral information. See RG 203.119.

5. The mortgage broker, not the platform, is responsible for:
   - responsible lending assessments under Chapter 3 of the NCCP Act
   - providing the credit guide and credit proposal disclosure document
   - ensuring the credit contract is not unsuitable for the consumer
   - all other obligations of a credit assistance provider

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] The platform's referral process must be reviewed by legal counsel specialising in credit law to ensure it stays within the mere referral exemption and does not inadvertently constitute credit assistance.

## Sections requiring legal review

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Overall disclosure framework:** This entire document must be reviewed by a solicitor qualified in Australian financial services, credit and consumer law before publication. The disclosure language must be tailored to the platform's specific referral arrangements, fee structures and business model.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Referral fee accuracy:** The description of referral fee structures must be verified against actual signed referral agreements. Misleading descriptions of fee arrangements would breach s18 of the ACL.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Partner vetting claims:** Claims about partner selection criteria must be verified. If the platform does not in fact vet partners against the stated criteria, those claims must be removed or amended.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Credit activity boundary:** The platform's referral process must be reviewed to ensure it does not cross the line from mere referral into credit assistance. This includes reviewing all user-facing content, scripts, chat flows and automated messages. See RG 203.117.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Upstream referral agreements:** If the platform engages in upstream referrals (passing consumer details to licensees), written referral agreements must be in place and the platform must confirm that licensees are complying with their additional licence conditions under reg 9AB of the National Credit Regulations.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **Comparison service characterisation:** Legal advice is needed on whether the platform constitutes a "comparison service" under the National Credit Act or ACL. This affects the disclosure obligations that apply.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] **AFS licence implications:** If the platform discusses financial products in a way that could be characterised as providing financial product advice (even general advice), legal advice is needed on whether an AFS licence is required. See ASIC RG 36.
