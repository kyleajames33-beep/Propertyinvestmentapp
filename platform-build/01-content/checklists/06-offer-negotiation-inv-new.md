# Offer & Negotiation Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Comparable sales and pricing
- [ ] Analyse at least 5 comparable sales from the last 6 months: same suburb, similar size and type
- [ ] Calculate the price per square metre for land and per square metre for building to benchmark
- [ ] Adjust comparables for condition, orientation, street position, and rental income
- [ ] Determine the vendor's motivation: investor sell-off, estate sale, mortgagee, or developer close-out
- [ ] Assess days on market: properties listed over 60 days typically have more negotiable vendors

## Offer strategy and terms
- [ ] Set your maximum offer based on your numbers, not emotion: if the yield does not stack up, walk away
- [ ] Prepare a written offer with price, deposit (typically 10%), settlement period, and conditions
- [ ] Include a finance clause contingent on your investment loan approval
- [ ] Include a building and pest inspection clause unless you have already completed satisfactory inspections
- [ ] Include a subject to lease clause if the property is tenanted and you want to verify the lease terms
- [ ] Consider offering a shorter or longer settlement to match the vendor's needs: this can beat a higher price
- [ ] Set an expiry on your offer: 48 hours forces a decision and prevents the agent shopping your offer

## Negotiation approach
- [ ] Open below your maximum: leave room to move up without hitting your ceiling immediately
- [ ] Justify your offer with comparable sales data, not just your budget
- [ ] Do not reveal your maximum to the agent: they work for the vendor
- [ ] Counter quickly but never immediately: a few hours shows you are considering, not desperate
- [ ] If the agent claims multiple offers, ask for evidence: written offers, not just verbal claims
- [ ] Be prepared to walk away: your next deal is more important than this one

## Auction considerations
- [ ] Only attend auctions if you are fully pre-approved and can go unconditional
- [ ] Have your deposit funds accessible: 10% payable on the day
- [ ] Set your maximum bid before you arrive and do not exceed it
- [ ] Consider using a buyer's agent or professional bidder if you are inexperienced
- [ ] If the property passes in, negotiate only if the reserve is within your maximum range

## Contract conditions
- [ ] Ensure a finance clause is included if not buying at auction: typically 14-21 days
- [ ] Include a due diligence clause for tenancy verification, strata records, and any outstanding issues
- [ ] Confirm the deposit structure: 10% on exchange or a reduced deposit if negotiated
- [ ] Negotiate the settlement period to align with your finance approval and any tenant notices
- [ ] Understand the cooling off period in NSW and whether it applies to your purchase method
