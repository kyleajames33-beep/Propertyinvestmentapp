# Financial product advice disclaimer template

## Purpose

This document provides financial product advice disclaimer statements for pages discussing lending, mortgages, credit products, investment property returns, and other financial topics. These disclaimers address the requirements of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth), the National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009 (Cth) and ASIC regulatory guidance.

Under the Corporations Act, a person must hold an Australian Financial Services (AFS) licence to provide financial product advice. Under the National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009, a person must hold an Australian Credit Licence (ACL) to engage in credit activities, including providing credit assistance. This platform does not hold either licence.

## Standard financial product advice disclaimer (200 words)

> The information on this page is educational and general in nature only. It does not constitute financial product advice under the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) and it should not be relied upon as such. [Platform Name] is not licensed to provide financial product advice under an Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL), nor does it hold an Australian Credit Licence (ACL). We do not provide personal advice that takes into account your objectives, financial situation or needs.
>
> Any discussion of lending, mortgages, credit products or investment returns on this page is for informational purposes only. It is not a recommendation to apply for, acquire or hold any financial product. If you need advice about a specific financial product or your lending options, you should speak with a licensed financial adviser or an Australian Credit Licence holder who can provide credit assistance under the National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009.
>
> The content on this page is prepared without considering your individual circumstances. Before making any decision about a financial product, you should read the relevant Product Disclosure Statement (PDS), Target Market Determination (TMD) and terms and conditions. You should also seek independent professional advice tailored to your situation.
>
> [Platform Name] may receive referral fees if you choose to engage with certain professionals through our platform. See our referral disclosure for details. Your engagement with any professional referred through this platform is entirely at your discretion.

**Placement:** Top of all pages discussing mortgages, loans, credit products, investment returns, or interest rates. Must appear before any specific product discussion.

## Specific disclaimer elements explained

### Why the AFS licence disclaimer is required

Section 911A of the Corporations Act prohibits a person from carrying on a financial services business unless they hold an AFS licence covering the provision of those financial services. Providing financial product advice is a financial service. See ASIC RG 36. If the platform is not licensed, it must not represent that it provides financial product advice. The disclaimer makes this position clear to users.

### Why the ACL disclaimer is required

Section 29 of the National Consumer Credit Protection Act prohibits a person from engaging in credit activities unless they are licensed or exempt. Credit activities include providing credit assistance. Under RG 203.65-RG 203.70, discussing particular credit products in a way that suggests or assists a consumer to apply may constitute credit assistance. The platform must clarify that it does not provide credit assistance and relies on referral exemptions.

### The downstream referral exemption

Under reg 25(2) and reg 25(2A) of the National Credit Regulations, a person who refers a consumer to a credit licensee or representative and gives the consumer information about how to contact the licensee may be exempt from the licensing requirement. A condition of this exemption is that the referrer discloses to the consumer any benefits they may receive for giving the referral, in the same form as the referral information. See RG 203.119.

### Product Disclosure Statements and Target Market Determinations

Since October 2021, issuers of financial products are required to make a Target Market Determination (TMD) for each product. The TMD describes the class of consumers for whom the product is likely to be appropriate. Users should be directed to review both the PDS and TMD before making any decision about a financial product.

## Notes for implementation

- The financial product advice disclaimer must appear before any discussion of specific mortgage products, interest rates or investment returns.
- It must be visually distinct from the surrounding content (callout box, different background colour or border).
- The disclaimer must link to the dedicated referral disclosure page and the general disclaimer page.
- If the platform adds mortgage comparison tools, calculators or other interactive features that generate specific outputs, legal advice must be obtained to ensure the output does not constitute financial product advice or credit assistance. See RG 203.69-RG 203.70 on what constitutes "assisting" a consumer to apply for credit.
- All references to interest rates, fees or product features must include the date the information was last updated.

## Sections requiring legal review

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] The entire financial product advice disclaimer must be reviewed by a solicitor with expertise in Australian financial services regulation and credit law before publication. The disclaimer must be tailored to the platform's specific activities and business model.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] Any interactive tools, calculators or comparison features must be assessed by legal counsel to determine whether they constitute financial product advice or credit assistance. If they do, the platform must either obtain the appropriate licences or modify the tools.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] The specific wording of the referral fee disclosure must be checked against the actual referral agreements to ensure it accurately describes the nature and amount of benefits received. This is a condition of the downstream referral exemption under reg 25(2) of the National Credit Regulations.

[REQUIRES LEGAL REVIEW] If the platform enters into upstream referral arrangements (where consumer contact details are passed to a licensee), a written referral agreement must be in place and additional licence conditions apply to the licensee under reg 9AB of the National Credit Regulations. Legal review is required.
