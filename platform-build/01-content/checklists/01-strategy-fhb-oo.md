# Strategy Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Financial readiness
- [ ] Tally your total deposit savings across all accounts and term deposits
- [ ] Calculate your loan-to-value ratio (LVR) using deposit divided by target purchase price
- [ ] Budget for stamp duty using NSW government's online calculator
- [ ] Add up all upfront costs: LMI, conveyancing, building inspection, pest inspection, connection fees, moving costs
- [ ] Confirm you have a genuine savings buffer of at least 3 months of repayments left after purchase
- [ ] Check your credit score via Equifax, Experian or illion and note any issues to resolve

## Scheme eligibility
- [ ] Check if you qualify for the First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) in NSW or territory
- [ ] Research the First Home Guarantee (formerly FHLDS) spots and application windows
- [ ] Investigate the Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee if buying in a regional area
- [ ] Check NSW's first home buyer stamp duty concession or exemption thresholds
- [ ] Confirm you meet the property value caps for any scheme you plan to use
- [ ] Check if NSW offers a shared equity scheme and whether you qualify

## Buying criteria
- [ ] List your non-negotiables: minimum bedrooms, bathrooms, parking, proximity to work or family
- [ ] List your nice-to-haves: deck, study, backyard size, storage, natural light
- [ ] Set a hard upper price limit and write it down where you will see it during the search
- [ ] Decide your preferred property type: house, townhouse, apartment, villa: and why
- [ ] Define your target suburbs and create a ranked shortlist of at least 5 areas
- [ ] Set a target purchase timeline with a realistic settlement goal date

## Emotional guardrails
- [ ] Identify what would trigger you to overextend financially and write it down
- [ ] Agree with any co-buyer on your walk-away price before you inspect anything
- [ ] Plan how you will handle auction pressure: proxy, bidding strategy, or avoiding auctions entirely
- [ ] Set a rule: no offers made within 24 hours of first inspection
- [ ] Commit to getting a building and pest inspection before exchange on any property

## Team assembly
- [ ] Research and shortlist at least 3 mortgage brokers or lenders to speak with
- [ ] Book an initial call with a conveyancer or solicitor to understand the buying process
- [ ] Identify a buyer's agent if you plan to use one: research fees and inclusions
- [ ] Ask friends or family for recommendations on building inspectors in your target area
