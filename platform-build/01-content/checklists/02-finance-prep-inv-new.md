# Finance Prep Checklist: First-Time Investor

## Document gathering
- [ ] Collect the last 3 months of payslips or your tax returns if self-employed
- [ ] Download your last 2 Notices of Assessment from myGov
- [ ] Gather 3 months of statements for all bank accounts, credit cards, and existing loans
- [ ] Compile evidence of any additional income: rental statements, dividends, freelance work
- [ ] Prepare your deposit evidence: savings, equity release, or gift documentation
- [ ] Collect identity documents: driver licence and passport or Medicare card
- [ ] Organise a property valuation on your existing home if accessing equity for the deposit

## Credit and borrowing position
- [ ] Pull your credit report from all 3 bureaus: Equifax, Experian, illion
- [ ] Dispute any errors and pay down credit card balances below 30% of limits
- [ ] Close unused credit facilities and buy-now-pay-later accounts
- [ ] Calculate your debt-to-income ratio and confirm it sits under lender thresholds
- [ ] Avoid any new credit applications for 3 months before your investment loan application
- [ ] Confirm your existing home loan allows equity release without triggering a full reassessment

## Structure and tax setup
- [ ] Confirm your ownership structure with your accountant: personal, trust, joint, or company
- [ ] Apply for a tax file number (TFN) for the entity if not already held
- [ ] Set up a separate bank account for the investment property to quarantine rental income and expenses
- [ ] Research whether you need an Australian Business Number (ABN) for your structure
- [ ] Budget for annual tax return and accounting costs specific to the investment

## Broker and lender preparation
- [ ] Shortlist brokers experienced with investment lending, not just owner-occupier loans
- [ ] Ask each broker about investor-specific policies: interest-only availability, servicing buffers, rate premiums
- [ ] Request a comparison of at least 5 investment loan products including comparison rates
- [ ] Compare interest-only versus principal-and-interest repayments over a 5-year horizon
- [ ] Ask about loan features: offset accounts, redraw facilities, portability, and split loan options
- [ ] Understand how rental income is factored into servicing calculations

## Pre-approval and equity
- [ ] Apply for pre-approval with your chosen lender through your broker
- [ ] If using equity, complete the loan increase or line of credit application simultaneously
- [ ] Confirm the pre-approval covers investment purchases in your target state
- [ ] Check the maximum LVR for investment loans: typically 80-90% with LMI
- [ ] Get written confirmation of the pre-approval, expiry date, and attached conditions
- [ ] Stress-test your cash flow: model a 3% rate increase and 4-week vacancy to confirm you can hold
