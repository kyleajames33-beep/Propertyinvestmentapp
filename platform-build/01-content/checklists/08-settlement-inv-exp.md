# Settlement Checklist: Experienced Investor

## Settlement execution
- [ ] Confirm settlement time and fund availability with your conveyancer
- [ ] Verify the settlement statement: rate adjustments, strata levies, rent apportionment
- [ ] Be available by phone on settlement day for any issues
- [ ] Confirm settlement completion and collect keys or arrange access

## Tenancy and management transition
- [ ] Hand over tenant details, lease, and bond transfer to your property manager
- [ ] Confirm rent payment method continuity and update if needed
- [ ] Instruct the manager on your maintenance approval threshold and preferred tradies
- [ ] Review the property management agreement and fee structure before signing
- [ ] If changing managers, coordinate the handover to avoid service gaps

## Insurance and risk
- [ ] Confirm building and landlord insurance activated from exchange: entity matches ownership structure
- [ ] Provide certificate of currency to conveyancer pre-settlement
- [ ] Review coverage adequacy against replacement cost: adjust if needed post-settlement

## Portfolio integration
- [ ] Update your portfolio tracking spreadsheet with the new acquisition: purchase price, loan, rent, costs
- [ ] Order a depreciation schedule from a quantity surveyor immediately
- [ ] Set up the property's dedicated bank account for income and expense quarantine
- [ ] Automate loan repayments and diarise rate review dates
- [ ] Confirm correct title transfer with a post-settlement title search
- [ ] Update your net worth statement and LVR calculations

## 90-day optimisation
- [ ] Review first quarter performance: rent received, expenses, actual versus projected yield
- [ ] Identify any immediate maintenance or value-add opportunities
- [ ] Confirm your accountant has captured the purchase in your tax structuring
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for lease expiry, rent review, insurance renewal, and rates payments
