# Finance Prep Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Document gathering
- [ ] Gather the last 3 months of payslips from your employer
- [ ] Download your last 2 years of tax returns and Notice of Assessment from myGov
- [ ] Collect 3 months of bank statements for all accounts you will declare to the lender
- [ ] Print your employment contract or a letter from your employer confirming your role and tenure
- [ ] Compile statements for any existing loans, credit cards, or buy-now-pay-later accounts
- [ ] Gather your driver licence, passport, or Medicare card for identity verification
- [ ] Prepare evidence of your deposit source: savings statements, gift letter, or First Home Super Saver release
- [ ] Collect your rental ledger or board payment records if they support your savings history
- [ ] Organise all documents into a single folder: physical and digital: so you can respond to lender requests quickly

## Credit file clean-up
- [ ] Request your free credit report from Equifax, Experian and illion
- [ ] Check every entry for errors: incorrect defaults, duplicate accounts, or outdated listings
- [ ] Dispute any errors directly with the credit reporting body and the credit provider
- [ ] Pay down credit card balances to under 30% of your limit: ideally under 20%
- [ ] Close any buy-now-pay-later accounts you do not need active
- [ ] Avoid applying for any new credit, phone plans, or car loans in the 3 months before your home loan application
- [ ] Set up automatic minimum repayments on all existing debts so you never miss a payment

## Expense reduction
- [ ] Track every dollar you spend for 2 weeks to identify leaks
- [ ] Cancel unused subscriptions: streaming services, gym memberships, apps
- [ ] Reduce discretionary spending and redirect the savings into your deposit account
- [ ] Show consistent savings behaviour for at least 3 months before applying: lenders review transaction histories
- [ ] Avoid large cash withdrawals that are hard to explain on NSWments

## Broker and lender preparation
- [ ] Research at least 3 mortgage brokers and book a no-obligation initial consultation
- [ ] Ask each broker how many lenders they have on their panel and how they are paid
- [ ] Prepare a written summary of your income, deposit, target price range, and preferred loan features
- [ ] Ask about lender-specific policies that could affect you: casual employment, probation, self-employed parents
- [ ] Compare at least 2 lenders directly even if you use a broker: know the market rate
- [ ] Ask about comparison rates, not just headline rates: the true cost includes fees

## Pre-approval application
- [ ] Choose between pre-approval (conditional) and full approval pathways based on your situation
- [ ] Submit your application with all supporting documents to avoid delays and multiple credit checks
- [ ] Ask your broker or lender how long the pre-approval is valid for: usually 90 days
- [ ] Confirm whether the pre-approval includes a credit check and whether it is a hard or soft inquiry
- [ ] Get the pre-approval in writing and check the conditions attached
- [ ] Understand what happens if your pre-approval expires before you find a property
