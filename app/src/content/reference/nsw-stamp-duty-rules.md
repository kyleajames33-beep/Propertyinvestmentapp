# NSW Stamp Duty (Transfer Duty) Rules

> Last updated: July 2025. All rates reflect current thresholds unless otherwise stated. This guide is educational only and does not constitute financial or legal advice.

---

## What is transfer duty?

Transfer duty, commonly called stamp duty, is a tax levied by the NSW Government on property transactions. It is governed by the Duties Act 1997 (NSW) and is one of the largest upfront costs when purchasing property. Duty is assessed on the higher of the purchase price or the property's market (dutiable) value [Source: Revenue NSW].

---

## Standard transfer duty brackets and rates (from 1 July 2024)

NSW operates a progressive marginal rate system. The following rates apply to all property purchases regardless of whether the buyer is an owner-occupier or investor [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.32; Revenue NSW, 2024].

| Dutiable value range | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 to $17,000 | $1.25 per $100 (minimum $20) |
| $17,001 to $37,000 | $212 + $1.50 per $100 over $17,000 |
| $37,001 to $99,000 | $512 + $1.75 per $100 over $37,000 |
| $99,001 to $372,000 | $1,597 + $3.50 per $100 over $99,000 |
| $372,001 to $1,240,000 | $11,152 + $4.50 per $100 over $372,000 |
| Over $1,240,000 | $50,212 + $5.50 per $100 over $1,240,000 |

Note: Different bracket thresholds may appear across sources due to rounding conventions. Always confirm precise calculations using the official Revenue NSW calculator [Source: Revenue NSW, 2024].

## Premium property duty

Residential properties exceeding the premium threshold attract a higher 7% marginal rate on the portion above the threshold. This applies only to residential property under Section 32A of the Duties Act 1997 [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.32A].

| Financial year | Premium threshold | Calculation formula |
|---|---|---|
| 2024-25 | $3,636,000 | $182,038 + 7% over threshold |
| 2025-26 | $3,721,000 | $186,667 + 7% over threshold |

The premium threshold is adjusted annually in line with the Sydney Consumer Price Index (CPI) [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

For mixed-use properties, premium duty applies only to the residential component. For land exceeding 2 hectares, premium duty is calculated on the proportion that 2 hectares bears to the total area [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.32A].

## First home buyer exemptions and concessions (First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme)

The First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme (FHBAS) provides transfer duty relief for eligible first home buyers [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Exemption thresholds

| Property type | Full exemption threshold | Concession threshold |
|---|---|---|
| New or existing home | Up to $800,000 | $800,001 to $1,000,000 |
| Vacant land | Up to $350,000 | $350,001 to $450,000 |

- Full exemption: no transfer duty payable for properties valued at $800,000 or less (saving up to approximately $31,090) [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].
- Partial concession: duty is calculated on a sliding scale for properties between $800,001 and $1,000,000 [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].
- Above $1,000,000: full transfer duty rates apply with no concession.

### Key eligibility criteria

To qualify for FHBAS, the buyer must [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]:

- Be 18 years or over.
- Be an Australian citizen or permanent resident (at least one applicant for joint purchases).
- Never have owned or co-owned residential property in Australia (with limited exceptions for property owned before 1 July 2000).
- Purchase the property as their principal place of residence.
- Move into the property within 12 months of settlement (or completion for new builds) and live there for at least 12 continuous months (6 months for contracts exchanged before 1 July 2023) [Source: Duties Act 1997; Revenue NSW, 2025].

### How to apply

Application is made through Revenue NSW, usually at settlement via your solicitor or conveyancer. The Purchaser/Transferee Declaration form must be completed as part of the process [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

## Foreign purchaser surcharge

Foreign persons acquiring residential property in NSW must pay surcharge purchaser duty (SPD) in addition to standard transfer duty [Source: Duties Act 1997, Chapter 2A; Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Surcharge rates

| Period | Surcharge rate |
|---|---|
| 21 June 2016 to 30 June 2017 | 4% |
| 1 July 2017 to 31 December 2024 | 8% |
| 1 January 2025 onwards | 9% |

The surcharge increased from 8% to 9% on 1 January 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25; Revenue NSW, 2025]. This applies to the full dutiable value of the residential property and is paid in addition to standard transfer duty.

### Who is a foreign person?

A foreign person generally includes [Source: Duties Act 1997, Chapter 2A]:

- **Individuals**: not an Australian citizen AND not ordinarily resident in Australia (requires 200+ days in Australia in the prior 12 months with no visa time limitations).
- **Corporations**: foreign persons hold 20% or more substantial interest, OR two or more foreign persons hold 40% or more aggregate interest.
- **Trusts**: the trustee is a foreign person, OR any beneficiary (including potential beneficiaries in a discretionary trust) is a foreign person.

### Exemptions from surcharge

The following are generally exempt from the foreign purchaser surcharge [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]:

- Australian citizens (including dual citizens).
- Permanent residents who are ordinarily resident (200+ days per year, no visa time limit).
- New Zealand citizens with Special Category Visa (subclass 444) who meet the 200-day residency requirement.
- Partner visa holders (subclass 309 or 820) meeting residence requirements.
- Retirement visa holders (subclass 405 or 410) meeting requirements.

**Important change**: International tax treaty exemptions for citizens of New Zealand, Finland, Germany, India, Japan, Switzerland, Norway, and South Africa were removed on 8 April 2024 due to federal legislative changes [Source: Federal Treasury; Revenue NSW, 2024].

### Discretionary trust trap

A discretionary trust is deemed a foreign trust for surcharge purposes if any potential beneficiary could be a foreign person, even if no foreign person has ever received a distribution. To avoid this, the trust deed must irrevocably exclude foreign beneficiaries [Source: State Revenue Legislation Further Amendment Act 2020 (NSW); Revenue NSW, 2020].

## Landholder duty (for company and trust acquisitions)

Landholder duty applies when you acquire a significant interest in a company or unit trust that holds NSW land with an unencumbered value of $2 million or more [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.158; Revenue NSW].

### Significant interest thresholds (from 1 February 2024)

| Entity type | Significant interest threshold |
|---|---|
| Private company | 50% or more |
| Private unit trust (non-registered wholesale) | 20% or more (reduced from 50%) |
| Registered wholesale unit trust | 50% or more |
| Public unit trust or listed company | 90% or more |

The reduction of the private unit trust threshold from 50% to 20% was enacted in the Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023 (NSW) and took effect on 1 February 2024 [Source: NSW Treasury, 2023].

Landholder duty is calculated by reference to the value of the landholding (including goods), effectively treating the acquisition as if it were a direct property purchase.

## Duty on mortgage transfers and refinancing

### Mortgage registration fee

In NSW, mortgage registration and discharge each attract a government fee of $175.70 [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Transfer fee

The property transfer fee (title transfer) is also $175.70 in NSW [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Is stamp duty payable on refinancing?

Generally, no transfer duty is payable when refinancing a home loan with a new lender, provided the property ownership does not change. The new lender simply registers a new mortgage and the old mortgage is discharged. Only the mortgage registration and discharge fees apply [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.67].

However, if refinancing involves a change of ownership (for example, adding or removing a person from the title), transfer duty may apply to that transfer [Source: Revenue NSW].

## Worked examples

### Example 1: FHB-OO buying $750,000 existing home

Jessica is a first home buyer purchasing an existing property for $750,000 as her principal place of residence.

- Standard transfer duty on $750,000: $11,152 + ($750,000 - $372,000) x 4.5% = $11,152 + $17,010 = $28,162
- Less: FHBAS full exemption (property under $800,000): $28,162
- **Transfer duty payable: $0**

Jessica saves $28,162 in transfer duty under FHBAS [Source: Revenue NSW FHBAS Calculator, 2025].

### Example 2: INV-NEW buying $650,000 investment property

Michael is an investor purchasing an established property for $650,000. He is not eligible for any first home buyer concessions.

- First $372,000: $11,152
- Remaining $278,000 x 4.5%: $12,510
- **Transfer duty payable: $23,662**

Michael must pay the full transfer duty of approximately $23,662 [Source: Revenue NSW Transfer Duty Calculator, 2025].

### Example 3: INV-EXP buying $1.4M investment via trust

Sarah is an experienced investor purchasing a $1,400,000 residential property through a discretionary trust. The trust deed has been amended to exclude foreign beneficiaries.

- Standard duty on $1,240,000: $50,212
- Remaining $160,000 x 5.5%: $8,800
- **Total transfer duty payable: $59,012**

Note: As the property is below the premium threshold of $3,721,000, standard rates apply. The trust does not trigger landholder duty as this is a direct property purchase, not an acquisition of units in a landholding entity [Source: Revenue NSW Transfer Duty Calculator, 2025].

## Timing: when duty must be paid

Transfer duty must be paid on the earlier of [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.25; Revenue NSW, 2025]:

1. Settlement (completion) of the property purchase, OR
2. Three months from the date of signing the contract.

For most standard residential purchases with a 42-day settlement, duty is paid at or before settlement. For off-the-plan purchases where the buyer intends to occupy the property as their principal place of residence, duty payment can be deferred for up to 15 months from the contract date (an additional 12 months) [Source: Duties Act 1997, s.49A; Revenue NSW, 2023].

Late payment attracts interest. The interest rate is set quarterly. For the period 1 April to 30 June 2025, the rate was 12.17% per annum [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

## Nominal duty increases (from 1 February 2024)

The Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023 increased various nominal duty amounts [Source: NSW Treasury, 2023]:

| Transaction type | Old duty | New duty (from 1 Feb 2024) |
|---|---|---|
| Counterparts and duplicates | $10 | $20 |
| Changes in trustee concession | $50 | $100 |
| Certain managed investment scheme transfers | $50 | $500 |
| Declarations of trust over unidentified property | $500 | $750 |

## Conflicting information noted

- **Bracket thresholds**: Some secondary sources (calculators, guides) show slightly different bracket boundaries (for example, $17,000 vs $17,001, $36,000 vs $37,000). The authoritative source is Revenue NSW. Where discrepancies exist between secondary sources, Revenue NSW figures have been used.
- **FHBAS maximum savings figure**: Some sources cite savings of "up to $21,529" while others cite "up to $31,090." The variation likely reflects different reference dates. The $31,090 figure applies to the current threshold structure for a property at exactly $800,000.
- **Foreign surcharge timing**: Some sources report the 9% rate as applying from "1 January 2025" while others state "2025 onwards." Both refer to the same change enacted in the 2024-25 State Budget.

## 2024/2025 threshold updates confirmed

- Foreign purchaser surcharge increased from 8% to 9% effective 1 January 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
- Premium property duty threshold increased to $3,721,000 for 2025-26 (from $3,636,000 in 2024-25) [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].
- Private unit trust landholder duty threshold reduced from 50% to 20% effective 1 February 2024 [Source: Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023].
- Corporate reconstruction concession reduced from 100% to 90% effective 1 February 2024 [Source: Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023].
- Nominal duty amounts increased effective 1 February 2024 [Source: Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023].
- Principal place of residence exemption threshold for land tax increased from 1% to 25% ownership interest, with transitional provisions to 31 December 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
