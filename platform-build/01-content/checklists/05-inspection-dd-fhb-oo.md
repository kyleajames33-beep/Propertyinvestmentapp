# Inspection & Due Diligence Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Building inspection
- [ ] Engage a licensed builder or surveyor: not the cheapest option, the most thorough one
- [ ] Request a pre-purchase building inspection report that covers structural integrity, roofing, plumbing, electrical, and moisture
- [ ] Ask the inspector to explain major defects versus minor maintenance items in person or over the phone
- [ ] Check for signs of subsidence: cracks in walls, uneven floors, doors that do not close properly
- [ ] Inspect the roof space for insulation quality, ventilation, leaks, and pest activity
- [ ] Check the subfloor or crawl space for damp, termite damage, and adequate ventilation
- [ ] Review the electrical board: is it modern with safety switches, or old ceramic fuses?
- [ ] Check the hot water system age and capacity: replacement costs $1,500-$3,500
- [ ] Ask about the condition of plumbing pipes: old galvanised steel or copper can be expensive to replace
- [ ] Get a cost estimate for any major defects so you can negotiate or walk away

## Pest inspection
- [ ] Book a separate pest inspection with a licensed pest controller: or a combined building and pest inspection
- [ ] Confirm the inspection includes termites, borers, fungal decay, and rodent activity
- [ ] Check whether the property has a current termite management system: chemical barrier or physical barrier
- [ ] Ask about the risk level for the area: some suburbs have higher termite activity than others
- [ ] Review the treatment history if the property has had previous termite issues
- [ ] Factor the cost of ongoing termite inspections: roughly $200-$400 annually: into your budget

## Strata or body corporate review (if applicable)
- [ ] Request the strata report or owners corporation records from a specialist strata searcher
- [ ] Check the quarterly levy amount and what it covers: building insurance, maintenance, sinking fund
- [ ] Review the sinking fund balance and the 10-year capital works plan
- [ ] Look for any special levies proposed or recently raised: these are additional costs on top of normal levies
- [ ] Check the minutes from the last 2 annual general meetings for disputes, maintenance issues, or planned works
- [ ] Review any outstanding maintenance or defect claims: especially in buildings under 7 years old
- [ ] Check the strata by-laws for pet restrictions, renovation approvals, and parking rules
- [ ] Confirm the building's insurance covers replacement cost and check when it was last valued

## Compliance and legal checks
- [ ] Obtain a Section 10.7 (2) and (5) certificate from council: it shows zoning, hazards, and restrictions
- [ ] Check whether any part of the property is heritage listed or in a conservation area
- [ ] Verify that all structures: decks, sheds, granny flats: have council approval and complying development certificates
- [ ] Check for easements, covenants, or restrictions on the title that limit what you can do
- [ ] Confirm the property boundaries match the title plan: compare the survey with fences and structures
- [ ] Check if the property has a swimming pool: pool safety certificates are mandatory in most states
- [ ] Verify smoke alarm compliance and whether any electrical or plumbing work has proper certificates

## Insurance and ongoing costs
- [ ] Get building insurance quotes for the property: compare at least 3 insurers
- [ ] Check if the property is in a flood zone, bushfire zone, or coastal erosion area: this affects premiums
- [ ] Obtain contents insurance quotes if you want coverage for your belongings
- [ ] Estimate annual council rates using the property's land value and the council's rate in the dollar
- [ ] Calculate water rates and any sewerage or drainage charges
- [ ] Estimate annual maintenance costs: budget 1% of the property value per year as a rule of thumb

## Final verification
- [ ] Drive past the property at night and on the weekend to check noise, traffic, and neighbour activity
- [ ] Knock on neighbours' doors and ask about the street: most people will be honest with a potential neighbour
- [ ] Search the address in local news archives for any incidents or controversies
- [ ] Verify the NBN connection type and speed at the address
- [ ] Confirm there are no proposed developments on adjacent blocks that could block views or create noise
