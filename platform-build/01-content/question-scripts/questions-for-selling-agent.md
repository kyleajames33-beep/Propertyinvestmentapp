# Questions to Ask a Selling Agent

## Why These Questions Matter

When you are selling a property, the agent you choose directly affects the sale price you achieve, the time it takes to sell, and the overall experience. A skilled agent markets your property effectively, negotiates strongly on your behalf, and keeps you informed throughout the campaign. A poor agent overpromises on price to win your listing, then chips away at your expectations once you are locked into an agency agreement.

A good answer includes specific comparable sales, a detailed marketing plan with costs, and a clear communication schedule. You are hiring this agent to sell your property for the best possible price. You are not asking for a favour. Interview at least two agents before signing anything.

---

## Before You Meet

Check these items before committing to a meeting:

- **Licensing:** In NSW, selling agents must hold a real estate agent licence or be a registered salesperson employed by a licensed agent. Verify on the NSW Fair Trading licence check.
- **Recent sales:** Look up the agent's recent sales in your suburb on Realestate.com.au and Domain. Check how many properties they have sold in your area in the last six months and whether the sale prices were close to the original listing prices.
- **Reviews:** Check Google Reviews and the agency's social media. Look for patterns in feedback about communication, honesty on price expectations, and how the agent handled the campaign.
- **Agency profile:** Check whether the agent works for a franchise or an independent agency. This affects marketing reach, fee structure, and the level of personal attention you will receive.

## Questions by Category

### Market Appraisal Method

1. How did you arrive at the estimated sale price you are recommending for my property? Walk me through the comparable sales you used and why you selected them.
2. What is the difference between your appraisal price and the price you would recommend we list at? How do you determine the listing price versus the reserve price?
3. If the market shifts during the campaign, at what point do you recommend adjusting the price, and by how much?

### Recent Comparable Sales

4. How many properties have you sold in my suburb in the last 12 months, and what was the average difference between the listing price and the final sale price?
5. Can you show me three comparable properties you have sold in the last six months that are similar to mine in size, condition, and location? What were the campaign lengths?
6. Of the properties you sold in my area recently, how many sold at auction versus private treaty? What was the auction clearance rate for your campaigns?

### Marketing Strategy

7. What is your specific marketing plan for my property? Which platforms will you use, what type of photography and videography do you recommend, and what is the total marketing budget?
8. Do you recommend professional staging or styling? If so, what is the cost, and can you show me before-and-after examples of staged properties you have sold?
9. How do you target buyers outside the local area or interstate? Do you have a database of active buyers looking for properties like mine?
10. What is your online marketing strategy? How do you ensure my property ranks prominently on Realestate.com.au and Domain?

### Commission Negotiation

11. What is your commission rate, and what does it include? Are there separate charges for administration, auctioneer fees, or additional marketing beyond the base plan?
12. Would you consider a tiered commission structure where you receive a higher rate if you sell above a target price? How would that target be set?
13. If I am not satisfied with the service or the campaign is not generating interest, what are my options for terminating the agency agreement, and what costs would I still owe?

### Auction vs Private Treaty

14. Based on my property type and the current market conditions, do you recommend auction or private treaty? What are the specific reasons for that recommendation?
15. If we proceed by auction, what happens if the property does not sell on the day? What is your strategy for the period immediately after a passed-in auction?
16. If we proceed by private treaty, what is your approach to managing multiple offers? How do you ensure I get the best outcome without breaching ethical obligations?

### Communication Frequency

17. How often will you provide me with feedback during the campaign? Will I receive a report after each inspection with buyer feedback and levels of interest?
18. If I have questions or concerns, will I deal with you directly or with an assistant? What is your typical response time to calls and emails?

### Sole vs Open Agency

19. Do you offer a sole agency or exclusive agency agreement? What is the difference in commission rate and service level between the two?
20. If I sign a sole agency agreement for a fixed term, what happens if I am not satisfied and want to switch agents before the term expires?

## Red Flags

Watch for these responses and consider them warning signs:

- **Gives you the highest appraisal price** without detailed comparable sales to support it. This is a common tactic to win listings, followed by price reductions later.
- **Cannot show you recent comparable sales** in your suburb for properties similar to yours. Local market knowledge is non-negotiable.
- **Marketing costs are vague** or pushed back until after you sign the agency agreement. Get the full marketing plan and costs in writing upfront.
- **Pressure to sign the agency agreement immediately**, especially with a long exclusive term. A confident agent gives you time to compare.
- **No clear plan for buyer feedback.** If they cannot tell you how they will report on inspections and buyer interest, they are not managing the campaign actively.
- **Unwilling to negotiate commission** or discuss a performance-based structure. While the cheapest agent is not always the best, inflexibility on fees can signal inflexibility on other matters.
- **Suggests a listing price that is 15% or more above recent comparable sales.** This extends the campaign, costs you more in marketing, and often results in a lower final price.

## Fee Structure

Ask these questions specifically about costs:

- What is your commission percentage, including GST? Is this calculated on the sale price or the listing price?
- What is the total estimated marketing cost, including photography, videography, staging, online listings, and print advertising? Which items are mandatory and which are optional?
- Do you charge an administration fee or other fixed charges in addition to commission and marketing?
- If the property does not sell during the campaign, am I liable for the full marketing cost regardless? Is any portion refundable?
- What is the auctioneer's fee if we proceed by auction? Is this included in your commission or charged separately?

## After the Meeting

1. **Request the agency agreement** and review the exclusive agency period, commission structure, and termination clauses. Consider having your solicitor or conveyancer review it before signing.
2. **Compare the appraisal and marketing plan** with at least one other agent. Significant differences in recommended listing price are a red flag that one agent is overpromising.
3. **Check the agent's recent sales** independently on Realestate.com.au. Verify the sale prices and days on market they quoted.
4. **Contact two recent sellers** who used this agent. Ask about communication, whether the sale price met expectations, and whether they would use the agent again.
5. **Negotiate the exclusive agency period.** Shorter terms keep the agent motivated and give you flexibility if the campaign is not performing.
