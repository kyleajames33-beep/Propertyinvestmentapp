# Contract Review Checklist: Experienced Investor

## Contract essentials
- [ ] Verify purchaser entity, property description, and purchase price are correct
- [ ] Review deposit terms: negotiate 5% if it helps portfolio cash flow
- [ ] Check settlement timing aligns with your portfolio cash flow calendar
- [ ] Confirm special conditions protect your position without creating deal-killer objections

## Tenancy and income continuity
- [ ] Verify lease terms, rent amount, and expiry date match what was represented
- [ ] Confirm bond is lodged and will transfer at settlement
- [ ] Check rent arrears status and any tribunal orders or disputes
- [ ] If vacant, confirm your ability to lease immediately and budget for vacancy period

## Risk and compliance
- [ ] Review title for easements, covenants, caveats, and mortgages to be discharged
- [ ] Check council certificates for zoning, hazards, and outstanding notices
- [ ] Order strata report if applicable: focus on defects, levies, and sinking fund health
- [ ] Verify no undisclosed special levies, disputes, or maintenance liabilities
- [ ] Confirm land tax clearance if buying from a taxable entity

## Special conditions and negotiation
- [ ] Keep conditions tight: finance and inspection only, minimal timeframes
- [ ] Add early access clause if you plan pre-settlement renovation or tenant preparation
- [ ] Include non-refundable deposit clause if appropriate for your negotiating position
- [ ] Flag any vendor-favourable clauses and push back before exchange

## Insurance and settlement readiness
- [ ] Activate building and landlord insurance from exchange: confirm coverage adequacy
- [ ] Provide certificate of currency to your conveyancer
- [ ] Confirm settlement funds will be available in the correct account on the day
- [ ] Review settlement statement and adjustments for rates, water, and strata levies before signing
