# Settlement Checklist: First Home Buyer (Owner-Occupier)

## Settlement day preparation
- [ ] Confirm the settlement date and time with your conveyancer at least 3 days prior
- [ ] Ensure your loan funds are ready and your lender has issued the loan documents
- [ ] Verify your deposit has been paid and held in the agent's or solicitor's trust account
- [ ] Prepare the balance of funds needed for settlement: stamp duty, legals, adjustments, and any shortfall
- [ ] Confirm the cheque or transfer details with your conveyancer the day before settlement
- [ ] Understand that settlement usually occurs between 12pm and 4pm: be available by phone all day

## Insurance activation
- [ ] Confirm your building insurance policy is active from the exchange date
- [ ] Provide a certificate of currency to your conveyancer before settlement
- [ ] Check that your policy covers the full replacement cost, not just market value
- [ ] Activate contents insurance effective from settlement date
- [ ] Store your insurance documents and policy numbers in a safe, accessible place

## Final inspection
- [ ] Schedule your final inspection within 48 hours before settlement
- [ ] Check that all inclusions listed in the contract are still present: appliances, curtains, light fittings
- [ ] Test all lights, power points, taps, toilets, heating, cooling, and kitchen appliances
- [ ] Check for any new damage since you last inspected: holes in walls, broken windows, stains
- [ ] Confirm the property is clean and all rubbish has been removed
- [ ] Take photos of the meter readings: electricity, gas, water: at the final inspection
- [ ] Check that keys, remotes, garage door openers, and mailbox keys are available for handover
- [ ] If you find issues, notify your conveyancer immediately: they may be able to hold funds at settlement

## Utilities and services connection
- [ ] Arrange electricity connection to start on settlement day: call the retailer 3-5 days prior
- [ ] Arrange gas connection if the property uses gas: some retailers need a technician visit
- [ ] Set up water account transfer: some states bill owners directly, others through tenants
- [ ] Arrange internet connection: book a technician if NBN needs a new installation
- [ ] Update your address with Australia Post and set up mail redirection for 3-6 months
- [ ] Notify your bank, employer, Medicare, and any subscription services of your new address

## Settlement day
- [ ] Keep your phone charged and be available to answer questions from your conveyancer
- [ ] Settlement happens electronically in most states: you do not need to attend in person
- [ ] Your conveyancer will confirm once settlement is complete and funds have been transferred
- [ ] The real estate agent will contact you once they have been advised settlement has occurred
- [ ] Collect the keys from the agent's office: usually late afternoon on settlement day
- [ ] Ask the agent for any alarm codes, gate remotes, strata access fobs, or mailbox keys

## Post-settlement tasks
- [ ] Change the locks on all external doors within the first week: previous owners, tradespeople, or neighbours may have keys
- [ ] Update the property address on your driver licence within 14 days: it is a legal requirement
- [ ] Register for council rates and confirm the billing cycle and payment method
- [ ] Set up a direct debit for your mortgage repayments: do not miss your first payment
- [ ] Test smoke alarms and replace batteries if needed: you are responsible from settlement
- [ ] Locate your water mains tap, electricity switchboard, and gas shut-off valve
- [ ] Introduce yourself to your neighbours: they are your best source of local knowledge
- [ ] Keep all settlement documents, loan papers, insurance policies, and warranties in a secure file
- [ ] Book any maintenance or renovations you planned: tradies book out weeks in advance
- [ ] Take meter readings on the day you move in and submit them to your retailers
- [ ] Celebrate: you have just bought your first home. Take a moment to enjoy it.
