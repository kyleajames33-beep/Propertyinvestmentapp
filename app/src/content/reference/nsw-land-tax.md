# NSW Land Tax Guide for Property Investors

> Last updated: July 2025. Investor-focused guide to NSW land tax obligations, thresholds, and surcharges. Educational content only, not financial or tax advice.

---

## What is land tax?

Land tax is an annual tax charged on the total taxable value of land you own in NSW as at midnight on 31 December each year. It is governed by the Land Tax Act 1956 (NSW) and the Land Tax Management Act 1956 (NSW) [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

Your principal place of residence is generally exempt from land tax. All other residential, commercial, and vacant land is potentially taxable once the combined value exceeds the threshold [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

---

## How land value is determined

### Valuer General land values

Revenue NSW uses unimproved land values provided annually by the NSW Valuer General. "Unimproved value" means the value of the land itself, excluding buildings and other improvements [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Three-year averaging

For land tax purposes, Revenue NSW calculates your liability by comparing the three-year average of your land value against the applicable threshold [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

| Year | Value determined on | Example land value |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 1 July 2023 | $1,050,000 |
| 2025 | 1 July 2024 | $1,100,000 |
| 2026 | 1 July 2025 | $1,150,000 |

Your 2026 land tax average: ($1,050,000 + $1,100,000 + $1,150,000) / 3 = $1,100,000

If this average exceeds the general threshold, land tax is payable on the amount above the threshold [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Land value vs. purchase price

Land tax is based on the Valuer General's unimproved land value, NOT what you paid for the property. This can be significantly different from the purchase price, particularly for properties with substantial improvements [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

## Land tax thresholds and rates (2024 onwards)

### Threshold freeze

The 2024-25 NSW State Budget announced a freeze on the general and premium land tax thresholds. From 1 January 2025, thresholds are fixed as follows [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25; Revenue NSW, 2025]:

| Threshold | Land value | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| General threshold | More than $1,075,000 but less than $6,571,000 | $100 + 1.6% of land value above $1,075,000 |
| Premium threshold | More than $6,571,000 | $88,036 + 2% of land value above $6,571,000 |

The thresholds are frozen until at least 2027, with a legislative review required by June 2027 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].

### Historical thresholds

| Tax year | General threshold | Premium threshold |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 onwards | $1,075,000 | $6,571,000 |
| 2023 | $969,000 | $5,925,000 |
| 2022 | $822,000 | $5,026,000 |
| 2021 | $755,000 | $4,616,000 |
| 2020 | $734,000 | $4,488,000 |

[Source: Revenue NSW, 2025]

### Why the freeze matters for investors

With thresholds frozen while property values continue to rise, more property owners will be pulled into the land tax net each year. An investor whose portfolio was below the threshold in 2023 may now find themselves liable for land tax as valuations increase [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].

---

## Premium land tax rate

Once the average taxable land value exceeds $6,571,000, the premium rate applies. The premium rate is $88,036 plus 2% of the land value above $6,571,000 [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

This affects high-value portfolios and luxury residential land holdings.

## Foreign owner land tax surcharge

### Surcharge rates

Foreign owners of residential land in NSW must pay surcharge land tax in addition to any general land tax. A tax-free threshold does NOT apply to surcharge land tax [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

| Land tax year | Surcharge rate |
|---|---|
| 2018 to 2022 | 2% |
| 2023 to 2024 | 4% |
| 2025 onwards | 5% |

The surcharge increased from 4% to 5% on 1 January 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25; Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Who is a foreign person for land tax?

The surcharge applies to [Source: Land Tax Act 1956 (NSW), s.5D; Revenue NSW, 2025]:

- Individual owners not ordinarily resident in Australia.
- Corporations incorporated outside Australia, or where foreign persons have substantial control.
- Trustees of trusts where a foreign person is a beneficiary (including potential beneficiaries in discretionary trusts).

### NZ citizens post-2023 changes

New Zealand citizens holding a Special Category Visa (subclass 444) who meet the 200-day residency requirement are generally exempt from the foreign surcharge. However, treaty-based exemptions for citizens of several countries (including New Zealand) were removed at the federal level on 8 April 2024. NZ citizens should confirm their current status [Source: Federal Treasury, 2024; Revenue NSW, 2024].

### Discretionary trust surcharge trap

A discretionary trust is deemed a foreign trust for surcharge land tax purposes unless the trust deed [Source: State Revenue Legislation Further Amendment Act 2020 (NSW); Revenue NSW, 2020]:

1. Excludes all foreign persons from being beneficiaries or potential beneficiaries (the "no foreign beneficiary" requirement), AND
2. Prevents the deed from being amended to allow a foreign person to become a beneficiary (the "no amendment" requirement).

Both requirements must be met BEFORE the land tax liability date of 31 December. Retrospective amendments do not remove surcharge liabilities [Source: Revenue NSW, 2020].

## Exemptions from land tax

### Principal place of residence

Your principal place of residence is generally exempt from land tax. Only one property can be claimed [Source: Land Tax Management Act 1956, s.10; Revenue NSW, 2025].

**Important change**: From 1 February 2024, the ownership threshold for the principal place of residence exemption increased from 1% to 25%. Only owners who hold at least a 25% interest (solely or combined) can claim the exemption. Transitional provisions allow existing claimants to continue until 31 December 2025 [Source: Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023 (NSW)].

### Primary production land

Land used for primary production is exempt if it meets the relevant use and area requirements [Source: Land Tax Management Act 1956, s.10AA; Revenue NSW].

### Boarding houses

Land used as a boarding house may be eligible for a concessional rate or exemption subject to meeting specific criteria [Source: Revenue NSW].

### Low-cost accommodation

Land used to provide low-cost accommodation may qualify for concessional treatment [Source: Revenue NSW].

### Other exemptions

- Land owned by charities and used for charitable purposes.
- Land used for retirement villages and aged care.
- Land subject to certain conservation agreements [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

## Trust surcharge and discretionary trust treatment

### Special trusts

A special trust does NOT receive the land tax-free threshold of $1,075,000. This means tax is payable from the first dollar of land value [Source: Land Tax Management Act 1956, s.3A; Revenue NSW, 2025].

Special trusts include:

- Family/discretionary trusts.
- Most unit trusts that are not "fixed trusts" for land tax purposes.
- Trusts created by a will (after a two-year period for discretionary testamentary trusts).

A special trust is taxed at a flat rate of 1.6% of the total taxable land value (up to the premium threshold), then 2% above that [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Fixed trusts and the threshold

For a trust to access the land tax-free threshold, it must be a "fixed trust" for NSW land tax purposes. This requires specific provisions in the trust deed that meet the legislative criteria [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

Bare trusts and self-managed superannuation funds generally receive the land tax threshold [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

### Surcharge land tax for trusts

Where a trust has foreign beneficiaries (including potential beneficiaries of a discretionary trust), surcharge land tax of 5% applies to the full value of residential land, regardless of the general land tax threshold [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

---

## Land tax in strata schemes

In strata schemes, land tax is assessed on the total land value of the scheme. Individual lot owners are generally not separately assessed for land tax on their unit. However, the owners corporation may be liable if the total value exceeds the threshold [Source: Revenue NSW].

This generally means that individual apartment owners within strata schemes are unlikely to receive separate land tax assessments unless they own multiple investment properties that collectively exceed the threshold [Source: Revenue NSW].

## Worked examples

### Example 1: INV-NEW with one $550,000 investment property

Tom is a new investor who owns one investment property with an unimproved land value of $550,000. He does not own any other property.

- Total taxable land value: $550,000
- General threshold: $1,075,000
- Taxable amount above threshold: $0
- **Land tax payable: $0**

Tom's land value is below the general threshold, so no land tax is payable [Source: Revenue NSW Land Tax Calculator, 2025].

### Example 2: INV-EXP with 3 properties across portfolio

Sarah is an experienced investor with three investment properties. The three-year average unimproved land values are:

| Property | Land value |
|---|---|
| Property A (Sydney apartment) | $850,000 |
| Property B (Newcastle house) | $520,000 |
| Property C (Wollongong unit) | $410,000 |
| **Total** | **$1,780,000** |

- Total taxable land value: $1,780,000
- Less general threshold: $1,075,000
- Taxable amount: $705,000
- Land tax: $100 + ($705,000 x 1.6%) = $100 + $11,280 = **$11,380**

Sarah's land tax liability is $11,380 for the 2025 land tax year [Source: Revenue NSW Land Tax Calculator, 2025].

### Example 3: Trust holding property worth $800,000 land value

ABC Pty Ltd ATF The Smith Family Trust is a discretionary trust that holds one residential investment property. The unimproved land value is $800,000. The trust deed has been amended to irrevocably exclude foreign beneficiaries.

- Land value: $800,000
- General threshold: Not applicable (special trust)
- Land tax: $800,000 x 1.6% = **$12,800**

Because the trust is a special trust (discretionary trust), it does not receive the land tax-free threshold. Land tax is payable from the first dollar [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

If the trust deed had NOT been amended to exclude foreign beneficiaries:
- General land tax: $12,800
- Surcharge land tax: $800,000 x 5% = $40,000
- **Total land tax: $52,800**

## Timing and payment

Land tax assessments are issued by Revenue NSW in the first quarter of each calendar year (typically January to March) based on land holdings as at midnight 31 December of the previous year. Payment is due by the date specified on the assessment, typically within 30-40 days of issue [Source: Revenue NSW, 2025].

## Conflicting information noted

- **Foreign surcharge rate**: Some sources report 4%, others report 5%. The 5% rate applies from the 2025 land tax year (based on 31 December 2024 holdings) onwards. The 4% rate applied for 2023 and 2024.
- **Trust threshold availability**: Some older sources suggest discretionary trusts "may" access the threshold if restructured. Under current Revenue NSW guidance, discretionary trusts are classified as special trusts and do not receive the threshold unless they are restructured as fixed trusts with specific legislative compliance.
- **Principal place of residence threshold**: The increase from 1% to 25% was announced in the 2023-24 Budget but has a transitional period ending 31 December 2025. Some sources may not reflect the transitional provisions.

## 2024/2025 threshold updates confirmed

- General threshold frozen at $1,075,000 from 1 January 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
- Premium threshold frozen at $6,571,000 from 1 January 2025 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
- Foreign owner surcharge increased from 4% to 5% from the 2025 land tax year [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
- Principal place of residence exemption ownership threshold increased from 1% to 25%, transitional period to 31 December 2025 [Source: Treasury and Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2023].
- Review of frozen thresholds required by June 2027 [Source: NSW State Budget, 2024-25].
